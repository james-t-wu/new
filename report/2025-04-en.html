Israeli merger and acquisition deals

1. Nyx Security Embedded systems security solutions

On 28th April 2025, Upwind, a cloud security provider based in the United States, has acquired Nyx Security, an Israeli embedded cybersecurity startup.

Nyx Security, established in 2024 by Gili Yankovich and Alon Saban in Tel Aviv, has developed innovative Application Detection and Response (ADR) technology that enables real-time monitoring of application function execution and precise identification of behavioral anomalies. The ten-person team contributes expertise in embedded systems and low-level research, having operated without prior external funding.

Upwind specializes in real-time cloud protection, delivering continuous visibility across infrastructure workloads. The company has established itself as a next-generation cloud security provider, emphasizing streamlined integration and comprehensive protection for modern cloud-native environments.

The strategic combination merges Upwind’s infrastructure security expertise with Nyx’s application-layer monitoring capabilities, creating an integrated platform that monitors both infrastructure and application behavior in real time. This union addresses the increasing complexity of runtime threats in cloud-native environments, where conventional security tools often prove inadequate. The unified solution enables customers to identify code-level anomalies and exploitation attempts while maintaining the simplicity of their existing technology stacks, positioning Upwind as an innovator in comprehensive cloud security coverage.

Application Detection and Response (ADR) is a security technology that monitors and analyzes the behavior of applications during runtime to detect vulnerabilities and anomalies. ADR tools provide real-time insights into how applications execute functions, allowing organizations to identify and mitigate potential threats effectively as they arise.

Runtime Security refers to the protection mechanisms implemented during the execution of applications to safeguard them against various threats and vulnerabilities. It involves monitoring application behavior in real time to detect and respond to potential exploits, ensuring that security measures remain active while the application is operating.

Similar Israeli companies include: Miggo Security, ARMO, Kodem Security.

2. Labrador Health At-home screening and chronic disease management

On 28th April 2025, Healthy.io, an Israel-based healthcare technology company, has acquired Labrador Health, a pioneering quantitative home testing startup. While the transaction amount remains undisclosed, the strategic acquisition aims to advance at-home kidney diagnostics through smartphone-based technology.

Labrador Health has developed groundbreaking technology for quantitative home testing, achieving remarkable technological milestones with a modest $5 million in funding. The company’s innovation lies in its ability to deliver precise, laboratory-grade diagnostic results in residential settings.

Healthy.io specializes in transforming smartphone cameras into clinical-grade diagnostic tools, offering FDA-cleared and CE-marked products globally. The company has demonstrated significant success with its semi-quantitative tests for enhancing early kidney disease detection.

The strategic merger combines Labrador’s quantitative testing expertise with Healthy.io’s advanced computer vision technology and operational infrastructure, positioning the unified entity to launch the first-ever quantitative home-based ACR test by 2026. This integration will significantly enhance accessibility to high-precision diagnostics worldwide, aligning seamlessly with existing care pathways and international reimbursement frameworks. The consolidated technology platform will enable earlier detection and improved outcomes for kidney disease management, particularly benefiting underserved communities where traditional testing methods have limited accessibility.

Quantitative Home Testing refers to diagnostic tests that allow patients to measure specific health metrics, such as kidney function, from the comfort of their own homes with high accuracy. This technology enhances healthcare accessibility and routine by providing lab-quality results without the need for traditional clinical settings.

ACR Test (Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio Test) is a medical test used to assess kidney function by measuring the amount of albumin (a type of protein) in urine compared to creatinine (a waste product). This test plays a critical role in the early detection of kidney disease and is particularly relevant for individuals at risk for chronic kidney conditions.

Similar Israeli companies include: TechnoPulm, ART Healthcare.

3. Lavie bio Microbiome-based agricultural biological products

On 21st April 2025, ICL has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire the majority of operations from Evogene Ltd.’s (Nasdaq: EVGN, TASE: EVGN) subsidiary, Lavie Bio, and Evogene’s MicroBoost AI for AG platform. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Lavie Bio is a pioneering ag-biologicals company specializing in microbiome-based agricultural products. The company utilizes its proprietary BDD technology platform and advanced computational systems to develop bio-stimulant and bio-pesticide solutions that enhance crop productivity and sustainability.

ICL, an Israel-based multinational company, has been strategically expanding its agricultural innovation portfolio through its ICL Planet division. The company’s successful collaboration with Lavie Bio over the past two years has focused on developing advanced bio-stimulant solutions for crops under various environmental stresses.

The acquisition encompasses Lavie Bio’s core team, technology assets, and commercial products, positioning ICL to accelerate the development of innovative agricultural biological solutions. The integration of Lavie Bio’s microbial expertise and cutting-edge platforms, including the MicroBoost AI for AG and BDD technology, will enhance ICL’s capabilities in sustainable agriculture solutions. This strategic merger represents a significant advancement in global agricultural innovation, particularly in microbiome-based products that address the increasing market demand for sustainable farming practices.

MicroBoost AI for AG is an advanced platform developed by Evogene that utilizes artificial intelligence and big data to enhance agricultural biological products. It optimizes the discovery and development processes of bio-stimulants and bio-pesticides, thereby improving agricultural productivity and sustainability.

BDD Technology Platform is a proprietary computational predictive platform utilized by Lavie Bio for developing microbiome-based agricultural products. It leverages big data and advanced informatics to enhance the discovery, optimization, and development processes of bio-stimulant and bio-pesticide products.

Similar Israeli companies include: IBI-Ag.

4. Simtex Travel technology and data integration

On 15th April 2025, Israeli tech company Voye Global announces the acquisition of Simtex, a fellow Israeli eSIM solutions provider, in a staged deal valued at $20 million, subject to undisclosed performance targets.

Simtex has established itself as a pioneering travel-tech platform specializing in eSIM technology, boasting partnerships with over 600 cellular data providers across 190 countries. The company has distinguished itself through strategic collaborations with airlines like LOT Polish and Condor, integrating data package sales directly into the flight booking process.

Voye Global emerged as a notable player in 2023, offering flexible international data packages through eSIM technology. The company’s innovative approach allows travelers to use a single data package across approximately 130 countries, with some plans featuring year-long validity periods.

The merger strengthens both companies’ positions in the competitive $100 billion roaming market. By combining Simtex’s extensive carrier partnerships and aviation industry connections with Voye’s dynamic pricing model, the unified entity aims to deliver more competitive and accessible connectivity solutions. The integration enables seamless data package purchases during flight bookings, creating a more streamlined travel experience and challenging established players in the global eSIM market.

eSIM technology enables the embedding of a SIM card into a device, allowing users to switch carriers and plans without needing a physical SIM card. It provides a more flexible and convenient way to connect to mobile networks, reducing the hassle of changing SIMs when traveling internationally.

Similar Israeli companies include: Keepgo.

5. SatixFy Satellite communication technology and solutions

On April 1, 2025, MDA Space Ltd. (Toronto Stock Exchange: MDA) announced its acquisition of SatixFy Communications Ltd. (NYSE American: SATX) for $193 million in an all-cash transaction, offering $2.10 per share for the Israel-based satellite communications company.

SatixFy is a pioneering provider of semiconductors and solutions for satellite communications, leveraging its proprietary chipset designs. The company has developed radiation-hardened digital beamforming technology that enables hundreds of satellite beams, significantly enhancing performance while reducing costs. With over 60 patents and a global workforce of 165 specialists, SatixFy has invested $270 million in research and development.

MDA Space, a Canadian company, serves as a trusted partner in the expanding global space industry, specializing in satellite systems and space technology. The company has established itself as a significant player in providing end-to-end satellite solutions and space-based services.

The acquisition strengthens MDA Space’s position in the rapidly growing digital satellite communications market, particularly in low Earth orbit (LEO) and medium Earth orbit (MEO) systems. The integration of SatixFy’s digital beamforming technology and satellite broadband capabilities will enhance MDA’s comprehensive satellite systems portfolio, addressing the increasing demand for advanced broadband and direct-to-device connectivity solutions.

Digital Beamformer is a technology that enables the generation of multiple beams in satellite communication systems. It improves performance by focusing signals in specific directions, thereby enhancing overall efficiency and capacity in satellite communications.

Satellites Broadband refers to high-speed internet access provided through satellite communication technology. This service allows users to connect to the internet from remote locations where conventional broadband infrastructure may not be available, using satellites to transmit data.

Similar Israeli companies include: Ayecka Communication Systems, Sky and Space Global, NSLComm.
