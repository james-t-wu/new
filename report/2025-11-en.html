Israeli Acquisition Events

1. Fordefi Secure digital asset management

On 25th November 2025, Paxos acquired blockchain security firm Fordefi for over $100 million, marking a significant expansion in the blockchain sector. The transaction reinforces Paxos' position as a leading player in the industry.

Fordefi is a specialized provider of institutional-grade security solutions for digital assets, focusing on protecting blockchain transactions and wallets for enterprise clients. The company has developed advanced security protocols that help institutions safely manage their digital asset operations.

Paxos is a blockchain infrastructure platform that enables the movement of assets across the digital economy. The company has established itself as a unicorn in the blockchain space, providing regulated solutions for tokenization, custody, and settlement services to financial institutions and enterprises.

Similar Israeli companies include: DLT-360 (Blockchain Solutions).

2. Roboteam Unmanned Ground Vehicles

On 25th November 2025, Ondas Holdings Inc. (NASDAQ: ONDS) entered into a definitive agreement to acquire Roboteam, a global developer of tactical unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs).

Roboteam specializes in rugged tactical ground robots used for explosive ordnance disposal, intelligence, surveillance, and hazardous-environment missions. Its UGVs have been deployed by defense and security customers across 30 countries, including the U.S. Department of Defense, Marine Corps, and Israeli Ministry of Defense.

Ondas Holdings provides autonomous aerial and ground robot intelligence through its Ondas Autonomous Systems business unit and private wireless solutions through Ondas Networks. The company is listed on NASDAQ and focuses on developing advanced technology solutions for defense, homeland security, and commercial applications.

Similar Israeli companies include: Elbit Systems (Unmanned systems and electro-optics); Octopus software (Security and crisis management solutions).

3. Galleon Financial management for gaming producers

On 18th November 2025, Stash acquired Tel-Aviv-based technology company Galleon to strengthen its direct-to-consumer commerce capabilities in games. The transaction amount has not been disclosed. This acquisition expands Stash's presence in Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Israel, with Galleon’s co-founders joining as senior executives.

Galleon, developed without external funding, began as an internal project at SuperPlay in 2024 before evolving into a full-fledged direct-to-consumer platform. The company’s technology is designed to handle hundreds of millions of dollars in annual transactions, offering native checkout experiences within games.

Stash is positioning itself as a definitive commerce powerhouse for gaming, aiming to redefine player experiences through seamless in-game purchasing.

The strategic combination enhances Stash’s ability to deliver high-conversion commerce experiences at scale while aligning monetization with gameplay. By incorporating Galleon’s studio-born technology, Stash strengthens its direct-to-consumer capabilities, particularly in casual gaming studios where native, frictionless purchasing experiences drive player engagement and revenue growth. This move reflects the growing importance of integrated commerce solutions in the gaming ecosystem, where direct-to-consumer and in-game commerce technologies are becoming increasingly valuable for studios seeking to maximize player lifetime value.

Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) is a business model where companies sell products directly to consumers, bypassing traditional retail intermediaries. This approach allows businesses greater control over their branding, pricing, and customer relationships, while also enabling them to gather direct feedback and data from customers.

Similar Israeli companies include: Carmel Direct (Outdoor cooking equipment and solutions); HyperGuest Ltd. (Direct distribution platform for hospitality); Inthegame (Interactive Overlay Technology Solutions).

4. Locusview Utility Construction Digital Management Solutions

On 18th November 2025, Itron (Nasdaq: ITRI) agreed to acquire Israeli startup Locusview for $525 million in a cash transaction to strengthen its digital infrastructure management capabilities. The transaction is expected to close in early 2026, subject to regulatory approvals across multiple jurisdictions.

Locusview, founded in 2014 by CEO Shahar Levi and President Alicia Farag, has developed a digital construction management platform that streamlines utility construction projects. The company has previously raised $75 million in funding, making this exit a significant return on investment.

Itron, a U.S.-based smart energy and water solutions provider, specializes in delivering innovative services and technologies for energy and water resource management. The company offers comprehensive solutions that enable utilities to optimize operations and infrastructure management across the grid.

This acquisition expands Itron's Resiliency Solutions portfolio by integrating Locusview’s platform, which has already managed over one million energy infrastructure projects in the United States. The deal allows Itron to extend deeper into the lifecycle of grid and network development as utilities accelerate investments in energy and water infrastructure. By combining their technologies, the merged entity will enable utility operators to plan more efficiently, execute construction faster, and maintain safer operations through real-time, data-driven decision-making across field operations and executive management.

Similar Israeli companies include: Just Manage (Construction management platform).

5. Akooda Innovative software solutions

On 18th November 2025, Tulip acquired Akooda, an Israel-based workflow intelligence company, in a deal valued in the tens of millions of dollars. The exact transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Akooda specializes in advanced data analytics and workflow management solutions that help organizations optimize their operations. The company’s technology provides comprehensive insights and streamlines business processes through intelligent data analysis.

Tulip, a manufacturing operations platform, offers solutions that enhance operational efficiency through digital transformation. The company’s technology enables manufacturers to improve productivity, quality, and agility in their production environments.

The acquisition strengthens Tulip’s position in the workflow intelligence market by integrating Akooda’s analytical capabilities with its existing platform. This strategic move will allow Tulip to deliver more robust insights and process optimization tools to its clients. Manufacturing clients can expect enhanced data-driven decision-making capabilities and more comprehensive workflow management solutions. The combined technologies address the growing market demand for integrated operational intelligence tools that bridge the gap between data collection and actionable insights.

Similar Israeli companies include: Briefery (Manufacturing operations management solutions).

6. Doti AI AI-powered workplace solutions

On 13th November 2025, Salesforce (NYSE: CRM) signed a definitive agreement to acquire Doti, an Israel-based agentic enterprise search company. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Doti AI specializes in unified search and knowledge discovery solutions, focusing on agentic enterprise search technology that enables more efficient information retrieval and utilization across organizational systems.

Salesforce, a global leader in customer relationship management software, continues to expand its artificial intelligence capabilities through strategic acquisitions. The company operates a significant AI research and development hub in Israel that will be strengthened by this acquisition.

The acquisition will accelerate Salesforce’s development of a unified, agentic search layer across its platform, with Slack serving as the conversational interface. This integration aims to create an environment where humans and AI agents can collaborate effectively, surfacing connected data and insights from across the enterprise. By incorporating Doti’s expertise, Salesforce will enhance its enterprise search foundation, making knowledge discovery more intuitive and efficient for customers. The transaction is expected to close in the fourth quarter of Salesforce’s fiscal year 2026.

Agentic search is a type of search technology that enables the intelligent retrieval and organization of information across various data sources, allowing both human users and AI agents to collaborate effectively. This technology enhances the user experience by providing a unified interface for accessing insights and data, streamlining workflows within enterprise environments.

Similar Israeli companies include: Beam (Intelligent Process Automation Solutions); Ask-AI (Enterprise productivity enhancement solutions); Usearch (AI-driven market intelligence platform).

7. Velocity AI-Powered Incident Response Solutions

On 10th November 2025, Israeli tech unicorn BigPanda signed an agreement to acquire Velocity, an AI-driven Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) and major incident response company. The deal values Velocity below the approximately $22 million it had previously raised, although the exact transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Velocity focuses on AI-powered SRE and incident response solutions that help enterprises automate incident detection and reduce operational workload. Its proprietary technology and engineering talent will be integrated into BigPanda’s core Agentic IT Operations platform, enhancing automated detection, response, and overall system reliability.

BigPanda, a leading provider of AI-driven IT operations automation, aims to transform traditional and manual IT operations into intelligent, automated workflows that improve system uptime while reducing operational costs. The acquisition of Velocity enables BigPanda to accelerate the expansion of its automation capabilities and strengthen its position within the enterprise IT operations market.

Similar Israeli companies include: Fair Value (Economic consulting and professional valuations); TWELVE ADVISORY (Advanced financial consulting and investment services).

8. eSelf AI Human-computer interaction and video conversation technology

On 5th November 2025, Kaltura (NASDAQ: KLTR) acquired Israeli AI avatar startup eSelf AI for $27 million, triggering a 30% surge in Kaltura’s shares. The transaction marks a strategic expansion into AI-driven personalized content delivery.

eSelf AI specializes in creating intelligent digital avatars that enable personalized user experiences across digital platforms. The company has developed proprietary technology that transforms static content into interactive, AI-powered representations tailored to individual users.

Kaltura, a video technology company, provides cloud-based solutions for video hosting, management, and distribution to enterprises, educational institutions, and media organizations worldwide. Founded in 2006, the company has established itself as a leading provider of video experience cloud solutions with a global customer base.

This acquisition significantly enhances Kaltura’s ability to offer AI-powered personalization within its video content delivery ecosystem. By integrating eSelf AI’s avatar technology, Kaltura can provide more engaging and interactive digital experiences, addressing the growing market demand for personalized content. The move strengthens Kaltura’s competitive position in the rapidly evolving digital content space, particularly as businesses increasingly seek sophisticated tools for user engagement.

AI Avatar refers to a digital representation of a user powered by artificial intelligence (AI) technologies. These avatars can be utilized across various applications, including personalized digital interactions, virtual assistants, and enhanced user engagement in digital content. The use of AI in creating avatars enables more dynamic and responsive interactions, making them a valuable tool in media and entertainment.

9. EVA Information Security Offensive Security Services

On 5th November 2025, Pentera acquired EVA Information Security, an Israel-based offensive security company specializing in AI red teaming and penetration testing. The financial terms of the transaction were not disclosed.

EVA Information Security focuses on offensive security services with expertise in AI infrastructure testing and application red teaming. The company provides targeted assessments for both internally developed and third-party applications, identifying vulnerabilities and logic flaws that may impact business operations and helping organizations evaluate the resilience of their AI ecosystems under real-world attack conditions.

Pentera, headquartered in Israel, is a market leader in AI-driven security validation, providing automated solutions that allow organizations to test security controls and reduce cyber-exposure risks at scale. Its platform continuously simulates real-world attack paths across traditional IT and cloud environments, enabling enterprises to validate their overall security posture.

The acquisition expands Pentera’s capabilities in adversarial testing for AI ecosystems by combining its automated security validation platform with EVA’s research-driven, expert-led red teaming services. This strategic move addresses the rapidly growing attack surface created by AI infrastructure and AI-integrated applications, enabling organizations to validate controls, mitigate risks, and demonstrate compliance within AI environments. The combined offering will deliver end-to-end security validation across AI-enabled applications, interfaces, and infrastructure, ensuring enterprises can protect their AI investments with the same rigor applied to traditional security programs.

The acquisition further enhances Pentera’s portfolio through EVA’s specialized services in AI red teaming and full-scope red teaming, giving customers the ability to assess their entire AI ecosystem—including MCP servers, chatbots, and AI-enhanced applications—to gain a holistic view of their defensive posture.

AI Red Teaming is a specialized form of offensive security testing that evaluates the security of AI infrastructure and applications by simulating real-world attack scenarios. This approach helps organizations identify vulnerabilities and weaknesses within their AI systems, ensuring strong defenses against potential threats.

Similar Israeli companies include: SafeBreach (Breach and Security Validation Solutions); Adversa AI (Adversarial AI security solutions).

10. Carbyne Cloud-native emergency communication response technologies

On 4th November 2025, Axon (NASDAQ: AXON) agreed to acquire Carbyne, an Israeli startup, for $625 million in cash. The transaction is expected to close in the first quarter of 2026, following Axon’s recent $100 million investment in the company.

Carbyne specializes in cloud-native emergency call management and operates centers across the United States, Mexico, and Israel. The company’s platform integrates real-time data and advanced communication technologies to enhance the efficiency of emergency response operations.

Axon, a leading public safety technology provider, develops and markets products including body cameras, digital evidence management systems, and emergency response solutions. The acquisition expands Axon’s growing portfolio of public safety technologies.

The deal serves as a cornerstone of Axon’s initiative to build a next-generation emergency communications network known as Axon 911. By integrating Carbyne’s cloud-native call-handling platform with Axon’s AI-driven insights, the combined entity aims to create a more efficient emergency response ecosystem. This acquisition follows Axon’s earlier purchase of Prepared, further strengthening its real-time capabilities in emergency dispatch.

A Cloud-Native Emergency Call-Handling Platform is a technology designed to manage emergency calls through a cloud-based system, enabling efficient communication and data handling during emergencies. The platform provides real-time updates and integrates with various emergency response tools to enhance operational effectiveness.

Similar Israeli companies include: Wave Guard Technologies (Mobile Big Data Analysis and Monitoring); SayVU (Emergency communication and safety platform).

11. Sentrycs Counter-drone solutions

On 4th November 2025, Ondas Holdings Inc. (NASDAQ: ONDS) completed its acquisition of Sentrycs, an Israel-based leader in counter-UAS network technology. The financial terms were not disclosed.

Sentrycs specializes in protocol-manipulation drone mitigation (Cyber-over-RF). Its systems, deployed in more than 25 countries, detect, identify, and take control of unauthorized drones without disrupting critical communications.

Ondas Holdings provides private wireless data and unmanned systems solutions through its business units and has been expanding its multi-domain defense technology capabilities, particularly in unmanned systems.

The acquisition significantly strengthens Ondas’ counter-UAS portfolio. By integrating Sentrycs’ proprietary technology with existing systems such as the Iron Drone Raider, Ondas aims to deliver comprehensive security solutions for urban environments and critical infrastructure facing increasing drone-related threats.

Similar Israeli companies include: ApolloShield (Counter-drone system solutions); D-Fend Solutions (Drone threat mitigation technology).

12. Anodot Cloud cost management solutions

On 4th November 2025, Glassbox acquired Anodot, a company specializing in business monitoring and analytics. The financial terms of the transaction were not disclosed.

Anodot is focused on real-time anomaly detection and autonomous business monitoring. Its machine-learning analytics engine automatically detects anomalies and business incidents across complex datasets, helping enterprises identify potential issues that could impact operations.

Glassbox is a digital experience analytics provider whose platform captures and analyzes customer interactions on websites and applications, reconstructing user journeys digitally. It delivers real-time insights and behavioral analytics to help organizations optimize user experience and business outcomes.

With this acquisition, Glassbox integrates Anodot’s AI-powered monitoring and analytics capabilities into its existing digital experience platform, strengthening its analytics portfolio. The combined system will cover both user behavior and operational performance metrics, enabling clients to monitor digital experience and business health in a unified view. Organizations using this enhanced platform will benefit from earlier anomaly detection and predictive insights, creating opportunities for proactive optimization across multiple digital touchpoints.

13. Orbit Communication Systems Communication solutions

On 4th November 2025, Kratos Defense & Security Solutions, Inc. (USA) signed a definitive agreement to acquire Israel-based Orbit Communication Systems Ltd (ORBI.TA) for $356.3 million, funded with cash from Kratos’ balance sheet.

Orbit Communication Systems is a leading global provider of mission-critical, satellite-based communication systems for mobile platforms, including aerial, seaborne, undersea, and land systems. The company serves major air forces, traditional prime contractors, and emerging defense and space companies across Israel, the United States, Europe, and the Pacific region.

Kratos Defense & Security Solutions is a technology company specializing in defense, national security, and global markets. Upon completion, Orbit will operate under Kratos’ Microwave Electronics Division, headquartered in Jerusalem, Israel.

The acquisition strengthens Kratos’ satellite communication capabilities, particularly for government and military applications. By integrating Orbit’s specialized hardware and systems with its existing portfolio, Kratos expands its technological offerings in mission-critical communication solutions. The deal reflects the ongoing consolidation trend in aerospace and defense as companies seek to enhance market presence and is expected to be immediately accretive across virtually all key financial metrics for Kratos.

Satellite-Based Communication Systems refer to communication technologies that use satellite networks to transmit data and information over long distances. These systems play a vital role in various applications, enabling connectivity in remote and underserved areas where terrestrial networks may be unavailable.

Similar Israeli companies include: Gilat Satellite Networks (Satellite communication and networking solutions).

Kypso develops AI agents that integrate with mainstream developer tools such as GitHub, Jira and Slack, enabling workflow automation without altering existing processes. Their technology aims to boost engineering team productivity by streamlining tasks and reducing manual effort.

Incredibuild is an AI-native developer platform focused on optimizing software development workflows. The company is well established as a leader in build-acceleration technologies, helping development teams increase efficiency in both cloud-based and on-premise environments.
