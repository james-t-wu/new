Israeli merger and acquisition deals in July 2025

1. ReturnGO Sustainable returns management solutions

On 31st July 2025, Global-e Online Ltd. (Nasdaq: GLBE) announced the acquisition of ReturnGO Ltd., a leading provider of AI-powered return and exchange solutions. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

ReturnGO has established itself as a prominent sustainable returns management platform, serving over 2,700 brands globally with its comprehensive post-purchase solutions. The company's platform excels in streamlining returns, exchanges, and automated tracking processes while maintaining a strong focus on environmental sustainability.

Global-e Online operates as a platform enabling direct-to-consumer e-commerce on a global scale. The company's technology infrastructure facilitates cross-border transactions and provides merchants with comprehensive solutions for international expansion.

The integration of ReturnGO's technology into Global-e's ecosystem will enhance its post-purchase capabilities, particularly in automated returns and exchanges management. This strategic acquisition strengthens the company's ability to deliver superior customer experiences in global commerce while addressing the increasing demand for efficient and sustainable return solutions. The technological synergy between the two companies aims to create a more seamless and environmentally conscious approach to international e-commerce operations.

AI-powered return and exchange solutions refer to technology systems that utilize artificial intelligence to automate and optimize the processes of returning and exchanging products in e-commerce. These solutions analyze customer behavior, predict return rates, and streamline return logistics, ultimately enhancing customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

Post-purchase solutions refer to services and technologies that enhance customer experience after a purchase. This includes managing returns, exchanges, customer feedback, and other interactions occurring after the transaction, which are critical for maintaining customer loyalty and satisfaction.

Similar Israeli companies include: OtailO (Returns Management Solutions); Yotpo (eCommerce retention marketing solutions).

2. Mirato Third Party Risk Management Solutions

On 28th July 2025, Sayari, a leading provider of risk intelligence from the United States, acquired Mirato, a pioneer in AI-based third-party risk management. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Mirato is a software platform leveraging artificial intelligence and natural language processing for third-party risk management. The company's technology has demonstrated exceptional performance, achieving over 90% accuracy with complete consistency in automating risk assessments, establishing itself as a recognized innovator in the fintech sector.

Sayari specializes in providing visibility into complex commercial networks through its global commerce graph, which encompasses more than 8.6 billion records across 250 jurisdictions. The company has established itself as a frontrunner in integrated risk intelligence, offering comprehensive solutions for supply chain transparency and risk management.

The integration combines Sayari's extensive global commerce data with Mirato's advanced workflow automation capabilities, creating a comprehensive risk management ecosystem. This strategic merger enhances the efficiency of third-party risk assessments while maintaining human oversight for critical decisions. The combined platform will utilize agentic AI to proactively identify anomalies and execute pre-approved responses, enabling risk professionals to focus on strategic analysis rather than routine assessments. The solution significantly strengthens organizations' ability to manage complex supply chain risks and conduct thorough due diligence across their business networks.

Third-party risk management (TPRM) is a systematic approach to identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks associated with third-party vendors or partners. TPRM involves evaluating the security, compliance, and operational practices of third parties to ensure they do not pose a risk to an organization's operations or reputation.

Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that have the capability to act autonomously based on their learning and pre-set guidelines. These systems can proactively identify anomalies and execute pre-approved responses, enhancing decision-making processes while allowing human experts to oversee and intervene when necessary.

Similar Israeli companies include: Panorays (Third-Party Cyber Risk Management).

3. Cynerio Healthcare Data and Device Cybersecurity Management

On 27th July 2025, Israeli cybersecurity unicorn Axonius has acquired Cynerio, an Israeli healthcare IoT security startup, in a deal valued at $180 million, with potential earn-outs that could elevate the total value to $250 million.

Cynerio, founded in 2018, specializes in developing advanced security solutions for protecting medical devices from cyber threats. The company has secured approximately $50 million in funding and maintains robust operations across Israel, the United States, and Europe, supported by a dedicated team of 70 professionals.

Axonius, established in 2017 and valued at $2.6 billion, delivers a comprehensive platform for managing and securing devices connected to corporate networks. The company has successfully raised $595 million to date and serves prominent clients including News Corp. and Schneider Electric. Notably, Axonius achieved a significant milestone by surpassing $100 million in annual recurring revenue in 2023.

The strategic integration of Cynerio's specialized medical device security expertise into Axonius' platform marks a significant expansion into healthcare and critical infrastructure sectors. This strategic acquisition positions Axonius to address the escalating security challenges in connected medical devices while broadening its capabilities in IoT and cyber-physical systems. The merger is projected to enhance Axonius' annual recurring revenue by tens of millions of dollars within the first year, while enabling Cynerio to leverage Axonius' established global network for scaling its solutions.

IoT cybersecurity refers to the protection of internet-connected devices and networks in the Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem from cyber threats. This includes securing devices such as medical equipment, home appliances, and industrial machinery, ensuring that they are not vulnerable to hacking or other malicious activities.

Cyber-physical systems (CPS) are integrations of computation, networking, and physical processes. They utilize sensors, software, and networking to monitor and control physical systems, enabling intelligent functionalities in various applications, including industrial automation and smart grids.

Similar Israeli companies include: ShieldIOT (Edge Device Security Solutions); Claroty (Cybersecurity solutions for cyber-physical systems).

4. Riskey Third-party risk management

On 17th July 2025, Vanta, a US-based AI-powered trust management platform, announced the acquisition of Riskey, an Israel-based pioneer in real-time third- and fourth-party risk monitoring. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Riskey specializes in continuous monitoring of vendor vulnerabilities, breaches, misconfigurations, and leaked credentials through its sophisticated AI scoring model. The company's technology efficiently categorizes findings and delivers actionable insights to security teams, enabling swift response protocols.

Vanta stands as a premier provider of automated trust management solutions, empowering organizations to scale their security programs while reducing operational overhead. According to IDC research, organizations implementing Vanta's platform achieve a remarkable 526% return on investment and realize annual cost savings exceeding $500,000 through streamlined audits and automated security reviews.

The strategic integration of Riskey's technology into Vanta's vendor risk management platform addresses critical challenges in third-party risk management, where research indicates 45% of organizations experience increased business disruptions due to cybersecurity incidents. This merger elevates traditional point-in-time assessments to continuous, intelligence-driven risk monitoring, facilitating proactive threat identification and rapid response capabilities. The unified solution delivers comprehensive vendor assessments through first-party security reviews and perpetual monitoring, optimizing risk management through automated artifact collection and sophisticated risk attribution.

Vendor risk management (VRM) is a systematic approach to managing the risks associated with third-party vendors. This includes assessing, monitoring, and mitigating potential risks posed by vendors to an organization's operations and information security. VRM strategies often involve continuous evaluation of vendor performance, compliance, and security postures to ensure that risks are identified and managed proactively.

Continuous monitoring is the ongoing assessment and analysis of an organization's security posture and operational risks in real-time. This approach allows organizations to identify and respond to potential threats and vulnerabilities as they arise, rather than relying solely on periodic assessments. Continuous monitoring is essential for maintaining up-to-date security defenses and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements.

Similar Israeli companies include: DeviceTotal (Security solutions for IoT and OT devices); Mesh Security (Cybersecurity and Zero Trust management); Cyber Observer (Continuous Control Monitoring solutions).

5. Dogiz Pet health management and activity tracking

On 9th July 2025, Vetted Pet Health announced the acquisition of Dogiz, an Israeli pet-health startup, acquiring its AI technology assets. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Dogiz is an innovative pet-health technology company that developed AI-powered tools for pet wellness monitoring, including the Dr. Poop analyzer for digestive health insights. The company's platform enables pet owners to track their dogs' fitness and earn rewards through their proprietary digital currency system.

Vetted Pet Health, which recently pivoted from operating brick-and-mortar veterinary clinics to a digital-first approach, specializes in tech-enabled preventive pet care. The company is transforming into a comprehensive digital pet wellness platform that emphasizes proactive health management through mobile applications.

The acquisition accelerates Vetted's digital transformation by incorporating Dogiz's advanced AI capabilities into their upcoming mobile app launch in August 2025. The integration brings features like the Dr. Poop analyzer and planned additions such as Dr. Teeth and Dr. Skin, enabling personalized pet care recommendations. The platform will implement Care Coins as a reward system for consistent pet care routines, redeemable for real-world benefits. This strategic merger positions Vetted to scale its user base rapidly while addressing the growing demand for sophisticated, accessible pet healthcare solutions.

Similar Israeli companies include: Dogiz (Pet health management and activity tracking).

6. Forwrd Predictive AI solutions

On 8th July 2025, Pendo, the world's first software experience management platform from the United States, announced the acquisition of Forwrd.ai, an Israel-based no-code predictive analytics company. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Forwrd.ai specializes in no-code predictive analytics solutions that enable business leaders to create custom AI models for predicting churn, upsell potential, and lead quality assessment. The platform distinguishes itself through its ability to deliver sophisticated predictive capabilities without requiring data science expertise.

Pendo has established itself as a pioneer in software experience management, providing organizations with comprehensive tools to understand and enhance user interactions with their products. The company's platform empowers businesses to collect and analyze user behavior data to improve product adoption and customer satisfaction.

The integration of Forwrd's technology will power Pendo Predict, a new AI-driven suite within the Pendo platform. This strategic acquisition enhances Pendo's capabilities in predictive analytics, enabling real-time identification of customer risks and opportunities. The combined solution transforms raw usage data into actionable insights, allowing teams to proactively address customer needs and drive revenue growth through features like churn risk modeling and expansion opportunity detection.

No-code predictive analytics is a technology that allows users to build predictive models and analyze data without needing to write code or have advanced programming skills. This enables business leaders to generate insights on customer behavior, such as churn risk and upsell potential, through automated processes and user-friendly interfaces.

Churn risk modeling is a predictive analysis technique used to identify customers who are likely to discontinue using a product or service. By analyzing various data points and user behaviors, businesses can anticipate potential churn and implement strategies to retain those customers.

Similar Israeli companies include: Noos AI (Deep Learning and Predictive Analytics).

7. SatixFy Satellite communication technology and solutions

On 1st July 2025, MDA Space Ltd. (TSX: MDA) from Canada has completed its acquisition of Israel-based SatixFy Communications Ltd. in an all-cash transaction valued at $280 million.

SatixFy specializes in developing next-generation satellite communication solutions, with a focus on space-grade chip technology including advanced processors and digital multi-beam chips. The company has demonstrated strong growth, doubling its revenue year-over-year to reach $4.9 million in the first quarter of 2025.

MDA Space, a leading provider of advanced technology and services to the global space industry, has established itself as a key player in satellite systems and space technology. The company's portfolio includes the Aurora satellite platform, which supports major projects like the Telesat Lightspeed constellation.

The integration of SatixFy's operations into MDA's Satellite Systems business area strengthens the combined entity's position in the growing market for software-defined digital satellite solutions. The acquisition brings valuable in-house chipset design capabilities to MDA Space, enhancing its technological autonomy and competitive edge in satellite communications. The synergy between SatixFy's chip technology and MDA's existing satellite platforms creates a more comprehensive solution for the rapidly expanding space industry.

SATCOM (satellite communication) is a technology that uses satellite systems to communicate and transmit data over long distances, providing connectivity for various applications including television broadcasting, internet access, and military communications.

A chipset refers to a collection of integrated circuits or chips that work together to manage the communication between various components of a system. In the context of satellite communication, chipsets are crucial for processing signals and enabling the operation of satellite devices.

Similar Israeli companies include: Ayecka Communication Systems (Satellite Communication Solutions); Ramon Chips (Space computing systems and semiconductors).
