Israeli merger and acquisition deals

1. APEX Generative AI Security Solutions

On 29th May 2025, Tenable Holdings, Inc. (TENB) announced its intention to acquire Apex Security, Inc., a US-based AI security innovator. The transaction amount has not been disclosed, and the deal is expected to close in the second quarter of 2025.

Founded in 2023, Apex Security quickly established itself as a pioneer in securing AI systems. The company focuses on managing AI usage, enforcing security policies, and ensuring large-scale compliance for developers and enterprise users.

Tenable, a leader in exposure management, has long helped organizations identify and mitigate cyber risks across their digital infrastructure. Its recently launched Tenable AI Aware currently supports thousands of organizations in detecting and assessing AI deployments across their technology environments.

This strategic acquisition aims to address the growing security challenges posed by the expanding AI attack surface, including shadow AI applications, AI-generated code, and unmonitored cloud services. By integrating Apex’s specialized AI security capabilities into the Tenable One platform, organizations will be empowered to proactively manage AI usage and comprehensively oversee the exposure of both deployed and developed AI systems. This integrated solution is expected to launch in the second half of 2025 and will further solidify Tenable’s market position in delivering holistic exposure management as AI adoption accelerates across industries.

Attack Surface refers to the total number of entry points (attack vectors) in a given computing environment where an unauthorized user (the attacker) may attempt to input or extract data.

Tenable One is an integrated exposure management platform developed by Tenable that provides a comprehensive view of an organization’s security posture across all assets and environments. The platform combines various security tools and capabilities to help organizations effectively identify, manage, and mitigate risks.

Similar Israeli companies include: Cynergy Cybersecurity, Applicure, XM Cyber.

2. Veriti Enterprise security posture management

On 27th May 2025, Check Point Software Technologies Ltd. (NASDAQ: CHKP) announced the acquisition of Veriti Cybersecurity, an Israeli cybersecurity startup. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Founded in 2021, Veriti pioneered the Preemptive Exposure Management category, offering an automated platform that identifies and mitigates security risks across multi-vendor environments. The company's solution integrates with over 70 security vendors, enabling real-time threat detection and automated virtual patching capabilities.

Check Point, established in Israel, is a global leader in cybersecurity solutions known for its Infinity Platform. The company has consistently demonstrated leadership in developing innovative security technologies and maintains a strong presence in enterprise network security.

The integration of Veriti's technology will enhance Check Point's Infinity Platform with AI-driven automation capabilities, enabling instant deployment of security measures across various environments. The acquisition strengthens Check Point's hybrid mesh security approach, allowing seamless remediation across multiple vendor platforms while reducing patching time from weeks to minutes. Combined with Check Point's External Risk Management solution, this addition creates a comprehensive framework for managing both internal and external security exposures.

Preemptive Exposure Management (PEM) is a proactive cybersecurity approach that continuously identifies, prioritizes, and remediates risks within an organization's multi-vendor environment. PEM emphasizes the use of automated solutions to detect vulnerabilities and apply virtual patches in real time, thereby assisting organizations in minimizing their attack surface without disrupting business operations.

Automated Virtual Patching is the process of automatically applying protective measures to software vulnerabilities without the need for traditional patching, significantly reducing the time required to secure systems. This technique enables immediate risk mitigation across various third-party tools by leveraging threat intelligence and existing security frameworks.

Similar Israeli companies include: Vicarius.

3. Vault Platform AI-driven compliance and ethics solutions

On 25th May 2025, Diligent, a leading provider of AI-powered governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) SaaS solutions from the United States, acquired Vault Platform, an AI-powered ethics and compliance solution provider. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

As a prominent player in the GRC SaaS market, Diligent leverages AI to strengthen corporate governance and compliance management. The company continues its strategic expansion, following its recent acquisition of Spark Compliance, a global expert in ethics and compliance program design.

The integration of Vault’s AI-first technology with Diligent’s GRC platform will enhance organizations’ ability to detect risks early and address compliance challenges more effectively. This strategic merger boosts Diligent’s global enterprise services through localized compliance functionalities and improved regulatory alignment, particularly with emerging frameworks such as the EU Whistleblowing Directive. The unified solution aims to transform organizational ethics management through AI-enhanced reporting systems, mobile apps, and web platforms, delivering deep insights into misconduct patterns.

Compliance Management Solutions are systems or software designed to help organizations comply with regulatory requirements and ethical standards. These tools support the monitoring, reporting, and management of compliance activities, enabling organizations to effectively reduce non-compliance risks and maintain operational integrity.

Similar Israeli companies include: Cigloo, Regulait.

4. Hour One AI-driven video content creation

On 23rd May 2025, Wix.com Ltd. (NASDAQ: WIX) has acquired Hour One, an Israeli generative AI media creation startup. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Hour One, founded in 2019, has developed a pioneering platform that enables the creation of studio-quality content at scale through AI technology. The company's proprietary cloud-based infrastructure combines generative AI inference with advanced 3D rendering to deliver personalized video and interactive experiences.

Wix, the Israel-based global website building platform, has established itself as a leading SaaS provider for website creation and management. The company has been strategically shifting towards AI-powered solutions to enhance its digital experience offerings and maintain its competitive edge in the web development market.

The acquisition strengthens Wix’s position in AI-powered digital experiences while reducing its dependency on third-party providers. The integration of Hour One's technology will enable Wix to offer advanced content creation capabilities directly within its platform, potentially revolutionizing how businesses and creators approach web design and content generation.

3D rendering is the process of generating a two-dimensional image from a three-dimensional model using computer software. This process involves simulating light, materials, and textures to create realistic or stylized visual representations, essential in fields such as animation, video games, and architectural visualization.

Similar Israeli companies include: Triple D Illustrations.

5. Suridata SaaS Security Posture Management

On 22nd May 2025, Fortinet (NASDAQ: FTNT) announced the acquisition of Israeli startup Suridata for an estimated tens of millions of dollars, enhancing its SaaS protection capabilities through Suridata's AI-powered platform.

Suridata, founded in 2019, specializes in SaaS Security Posture Management, developing AI-driven solutions that enable organizations to monitor and remediate risks across cloud-based applications.

Fortinet, a leading American cybersecurity company, has been strategically expanding its footprint in Israel's technology ecosystem, with previous acquisitions including EnSilo in 2019 and Perception Point for $100 million in December.

The integration of Suridata's technology will bolster Fortinet's Unified SASE offerings, providing enhanced visibility and control over SaaS environments. This strategic acquisition addresses the growing challenges of security management across multiple cloud-based applications, leveraging Suridata's advanced machine learning and natural language processing capabilities for automated risk detection and remediation in SaaS-first environments.

SaaS (Software as a Service) is a software distribution model in which applications are hosted by a service provider and made available to customers over the internet. Users can access the software without needing to install it on their local devices, allowing for easier updates, scalability, and collaboration.

Unified SASE (Secure Access Service Edge) is an architectural framework that integrates network security functions with wide area network (WAN) capabilities to support the secure access needs of users and devices, irrespective of their location. SASE solutions provide seamless and secure connectivity to applications and data in the cloud, ensuring consistent enforcement of security policies across all access points.

Similar Israeli companies include: Servara Solutions, Cato Networks, Talon Cyber Security.

6. Jounce AI systems installation and monitoring

On 13th May 2025, Red Hat, a subsidiary of IBM (NYSE: IBM), announced the acquisition of Israeli startup Jounce for $20 million, integrating Jounce's modular AI deployment platform into Red Hat's open-source ecosystem.

Jounce, founded in 2024, has developed a sophisticated platform for deploying, running, and monitoring complex AI systems. The company's modular architecture provides over 10,000 deployment options and adaptively responds to varying workloads, enabling organizations to scale AI operations efficiently and securely.

Red Hat, acquired by IBM in 2018 for $34 billion, specializes in enterprise Linux and hybrid cloud solutions. The company has established itself as a leading provider of open-source technologies for enterprise customers worldwide.

The integration of Jounce's technology into Red Hat's cloud infrastructure will enhance the company's AI deployment capabilities across multiple industries, including finance, healthcare, and enterprise technology. The platform's emphasis on flexibility and cost efficiency aligns with Red Hat's commitment to delivering scalable, secure solutions through its open-source framework.

Modular AI Deployment Platform is a flexible architecture designed for deploying, running, and monitoring complex artificial intelligence systems.

Open-Source Ecosystem refers to a collaborative network of software projects and communities developed and distributed under open-source licenses. This ecosystem enables developers to share, modify, and enhance software freely, fostering innovation and flexibility in technology development.

7. Opus Security Cloud Security Automation

On 13th May 2025, Orca Security, a leading agentless cloud security provider from Israel, announced the acquisition of Opus Security, an Israel-based startup. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Opus Security is a rapidly growing startup that specializes in agentic AI-driven automation and orchestration for cloud security remediation. Orca Security stands as a pioneer in agentless cloud security solutions, delivering comprehensive visibility and prioritization capabilities through its Cloud-Native Application Protection Platform. The company has established itself as an innovator in integrating generative AI capabilities within cloud security operations.

The acquisition marks a significant advancement in cloud security automation, combining Orca's established CNAPP capabilities with Opus's expertise in agentic AI-driven remediation. This strategic integration enables autonomous identification, prioritization, and resolution of cloud security issues at scale. The merger enhances Orca's ability to deliver real-time response capabilities while minimizing manual intervention in security operations. The combined technology stack particularly strengthens the platform's AI Security Posture Management capabilities, enabling organizations to secure their generative AI implementations across multiple cloud service providers.

Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems capable of autonomous decision-making and goal-directed behavior. Unlike traditional AI that passively processes input to generate output, agentic AI can initiate actions, plan over time, and adapt dynamically based on changing environments or feedback. These systems are especially valuable in cybersecurity, where proactive identification and mitigation of threats are critical.

Cloud-Native Application Protection Platform (CNAPP) is a security framework that provides comprehensive protection for cloud-native applications throughout their lifecycle. CNAPP integrates various security practices and tools to ensure visibility, compliance, vulnerability management, and risk mitigation in cloud environments, addressing the unique challenges presented by modern application architectures.

Similar Israeli companies include: Aqua Security.

8. Genoox Genomic data solutions

On 13th May 2025, QIAGEN (NYSE: QGEN; Frankfurt Prime Standard: QIA), headquartered in the Netherlands, announced its agreement to acquire Israel-based Genoox for $70 million in cash, with potential milestone payments of up to $10 million. This acquisition aims to enhance QIAGEN's clinical genomics portfolio by integrating Genoox's AI-powered software capabilities.

Genoox has developed Franklin, a cloud-based community platform that enables clinical laboratories to efficiently process complex genetic tests. The platform currently supports over 4,000 healthcare organizations in 50 countries and has facilitated more than 750,000 case interpretations, spanning applications from genetic disorder diagnosis to cancer treatment guidance.

As a global leader in molecular diagnostics and sample preparation technologies, QIAGEN, through its Digital Insights division, delivers comprehensive genomic interpretation solutions. Its portfolio includes authoritative databases such as the Human Gene Mutation Database and the Catalogue of Somatic Mutations in Cancer.

The integration of Franklin into QIAGEN’s Digital Insights portfolio marks a strategic enhancement in genetic interpretation capabilities. This synergy will strengthen next-generation sequencing (NGS) data analysis—from targeted panels to whole genome sequencing—while improving diagnostic yield and turnaround time for clinical laboratories. The acquisition is expected to contribute $5 million to QIAGEN’s revenue in 2025 and reinforce its position in serving small and mid-sized laboratories globally with AI-driven clinical decision support tools.

Franklin is an AI-powered cloud platform designed to support clinical decision-making in genetic diseases. It enables laboratories to analyze next-generation sequencing (NGS) data and delivers real-time, AI-driven insights to support clinical decisions across a range of applications, including genetic disorder diagnosis and cancer treatment guidance.

Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) is a high-throughput sequencing technology that enables the rapid sequencing of large amounts of DNA. NGS allows researchers and clinicians to sequence entire genomes, exomes, or targeted regions quickly and cost-effectively, facilitating advances in genomics and personalized medicine.

Similar Israeli companies include: FDNA, Leal Health, Sequentify, Foresee Genomics.

9. Keep Alert management in complex environments

On 7th May 2025, Elastic (NYSE: ESTC) announced its agreement to acquire Keep Alerting Ltd, an Israel-based open-source AIOps company. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Keep specializes in unifying and managing alerts through a single interface, offering both enterprise software and cloud services. The company's platform excels in deduplicating, correlating, and prioritizing events while automating root cause analysis through its workflow engine.

Elastic, recognized as the Search AI company, has built its reputation on the widely used Elasticsearch platform. The company provides comprehensive solutions in Observability, Security, and Search, leveraging AI and machine learning capabilities to process and analyze data at scale.

The integration will enhance Elastic's AI-powered automation capabilities across its product portfolio. Keep's AIOps technology will complement Elastic's existing AI/ML infrastructure, particularly benefiting from Elasticsearch's speed and scalability. The merger promises to deliver unified alert management and automated incident remediation, while maintaining Keep's open-source nature and integrating it seamlessly with Elasticsearch and Kibana.

AIOps (Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations) is a technology that employs machine learning and data science to automate and enhance IT operations. AIOps platforms analyze vast amounts of data from various IT operational tools and devices to detect and resolve issues, predict outages, and optimize performance, thus improving operational efficiency.

Workflow Automation (WFA) is the process of automating complex business processes and functions beyond traditional data manipulation and record-keeping activities. WFA systems streamline processes and improve efficiency by minimizing the need for manual input and oversight.

Similar Israeli companies include: BigPanda, Briefery.
