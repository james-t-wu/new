Israeli merger and acquisition deals

1. Lulav Space - Robotics software development and solutions

On March 27, 2025, ParaZero Technologies Ltd. (NASDAQ: PRZO) announced the signing of a non-binding letter of intent to acquire a 100% stake in Lulav Space Ltd., an Israeli company specializing in counter-drone systems and autonomous navigation technologies.

Founded in 2021, Lulav Space develops vision-based navigation solutions, including its flagship Autonomous Navigation Kit, designed for GPS-denied environments.

ParaZero Technologies is an aerospace company focused on safety solutions for both commercial and defense unmanned aerial systems (UAS). This acquisition is set to significantly enhance ParaZero’s capabilities in counter-drone systems, expand its presence in the defense sector, and leverage Lulav’s advanced autonomous navigation and drone interception technologies to explore new commercial opportunities.

Similar Israeli companies include: SkyLock, infiniDome, TrekAce.

2. Vanti AI - Smart Manufacturing Data Solutions

On March 17, 2025, U.S. technology company Tray.ai announced the acquisition of Israeli startup Vanti, in a deal valued in the tens of millions of dollars. The transaction will be carried out through a share swap.

Founded in 2019, Vanti has established operations in both the United States and Israel. The company focuses on developing AI agents aimed at optimizing manufacturing processes. Leveraging adaptive AI technology, Vanti offers no-code solutions that enable manufacturers to rapidly deploy predictive models. The company serves well-known clients across multiple industries, including automotive and pharmaceuticals.

This acquisition will significantly enhance Tray.ai’s capabilities by combining Vanti’s knowledge modeling with Tray.ai’s integration platform as a service (iPaaS). The synergy is expected to improve the adaptability and performance of AI agents in enterprise environments. The combined strengths of both companies will better position them to meet the evolving demands of AI-driven manufacturing solutions.

Similar Israeli companies include: Heat Intelligence, InsightZAP, Noble.

3. Inception XR - Virtual reality training in healthcare

On March 11, 2025, Relias, a diversified workforce empowerment platform based in the U.S., announced the acquisition of Israeli extended reality training solutions provider InceptionXR to enhance healthcare workforce training; financial terms were not disclosed.

Founded to serve healthcare professionals, InceptionXR specializes in customized virtual reality (VR) training applications, with an emphasis on immersive and scenario-based learning to advance skill proficiency and decision-making in clinical settings.

Serving over 12,000 healthcare institutions and 4.5 million caregivers, Relias provides comprehensive training solutions aimed at improving patient care outcomes.

Similar Israeli companies include: OtheReality, XRHealth, Surgical Theater.

4. OTORIO - Operational Technology Cybersecurity Solutions

On March 6, 2025, U.S.-based cybersecurity company Armis announced the acquisition of Israeli industrial cybersecurity firm Otorio for approximately $120 million, significantly enhancing its Operational Technology (OT) and Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) security capabilities.

Founded in 2018, Otorio specializes in securing critical sectors such as heavy industry, energy, and manufacturing, providing advanced solutions including secure remote access and attack path mapping, particularly effective in air-gapped and converged environments.

Armis, a global leader in cyber exposure management and risk mitigation, continues to aggressively expand its security product offerings. Integrating Otorio’s advanced technologies, including the Titan platform and autonomous secure navigation capabilities, will substantially strengthen Armis’ position in critical infrastructure protection, facilitating its strategic progression toward an IPO.

Similar Israeli companies include: SIGA OT Solutions, Claroty.

5. SoniVie - Hypertension treatment solutions

On March 3, 2025, Boston Scientific Corporation (NYSE: BSX) announced the acquisition of Israeli medical device company SoniVie Ltd. for up to $540 million to expand its presence in the interventional cardiology space. The transaction is expected to close in the first half of 2025.

SoniVie focuses on innovative solutions for hypertension treatment and has developed the TIVUS™ (Therapeutic Intra-Vascular Ultrasound) system. This investigational device is designed for renal denervation procedures and has shown promising potential in managing hypertension.

Boston Scientific is a global leader in medical devices, offering a broad portfolio of products and therapies aimed at improving patient health. The acquisition of SoniVie aligns with Boston Scientific’s strategic goal of expanding its interventional portfolio, particularly in the hypertension management field.

Similar Israeli companies include: Rimed, Resperate Inc.
