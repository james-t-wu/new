1. MitoCareX Bio Drug discovery targeting mitochondrial carriers for cancer treatment.

On February 26 2025, N2OFF, Inc. (NASDAQ:NITO), based in Israel, announced a definitive agreement to acquire MitoCareX Bio Ltd., a biotechnology firm specializing in computational drug discovery for cancer therapeutics, for $700,000 in cash and additional share exchanges.

MitoCareX focuses on developing therapies targeting the mitochondrial SLC25 protein family, utilizing advanced computational modeling to identify potential anti-cancer small molecules.

N2OFF mainly engages in clean technology solutions, including solar energy, energy storage, and agricultural technology, and this acquisition marks its entry into the biopharmaceutical field.

The mitochondrial SLC25 protein family refers to a group of transport proteins located in the inner membrane of mitochondria, which play crucial roles in metabolite transport and mitochondrial function. These proteins are involved in various metabolic pathways and are essential for cellular energy production and signaling.

Computational drug discovery refers to the use of computer-based methods and tools to identify, design, and optimize new pharmaceutical compounds. This approach integrates techniques from various fields, including chemistry, biology, and computer science, to accelerate the drug development process and enhance the efficacy and safety of new drugs.

Similar Israeli companies include: Minovia Therapeutics, Senseera Health, Anima Biotech.

NanoVibronix focuses on non-invasive therapeutic devices, aiming to enhance patient treatment outcomes through innovative medical solutions. The company is committed to advancing the healthcare industry by integrating new technologies that prioritize patient safety and efficiency.

3. Plantae by Huminn Innovative food systems solutions

On February 10, 2025, 80 Acres Farms, based in the United States, announced the acquisition of Plantae Biosciences, an Israeli biotechnology firm, for an undisclosed amount. This acquisition is part of a broader strategy involving capital raises totaling $115 million, aimed at enhancing controlled environment agriculture (CEA) technologies.

Plantae Biosciences specializes in innovative plant breeding technology, focusing on accelerated plant breeding methods developed in collaboration with the Weizmann Institute of Science. The company aims to revolutionize crop production by harnessing genetic advancements to improve agricultural outputs.

80 Acres Farms is a leader in vertical farming that employs cutting-edge technologies to produce sustainable, pesticide-free crops using renewable energy and significantly less water. The company has a strong retail presence, supplying fresh produce to over 1,500 locations across the eastern United States and operating through its technology-driven subsidiary, Infinite Acres.

This acquisition is poised to enhance 80 Acres Farms' capabilities, particularly in integrating advanced plant breeding technology into its operations. By merging these innovative approaches, the company aims to differentiate its product offerings in the competitive CEA market, providing crops that not only meet consumer demands for flavor and nutrition but also contribute to resilience against climate challenges. This strategic move signifies a commitment to advancing agricultural practices and expanding market reach through enhanced technological integration.

Controlled environment agriculture (CEA) is a method of growing plants in a controlled environment, utilizing technology to optimize conditions such as temperature, humidity, light, and nutrients. This approach enables year-round production, reduces resource usage, and enhances crop yields, making it a key innovation in sustainable agriculture.

Accelerated plant breeding refers to the use of innovative techniques, such as genetic engineering and advanced breeding methodologies, to enhance the rate at which new plant varieties are developed. This process focuses on improving desirable traits in crops, including yield, disease resistance, and climate resilience, ultimately contributing to food security.

Similar Israeli companies include: Grow-tec, Saffron Tech, PlantArcBio, BetterSeeds.

4. Balltime Video Analytics Solutions for Team Sports

On February 6, 2025, Hudl, the leading sports technology company, announced the acquisition of Balltime, a volleyball-focused analytics platform, the financial terms of the deal have not been disclosed.

Balltime, founded in 2022, leverages AI to enhance video analytics for volleyball, providing teams with rapid insights into performance metrics like serve speed and attack height.

Hudl, a leader in cloud-based sports technology, aims to improve its volleyball offerings and reach a broader audience of athletes and coaches through this acquisition.

As a leader in cloud-based sports technology, Hudl aims through this acquisition to enhance its volleyball products and reach a broader group of athletes and coaches. The integration of Balltime’s artificial intelligence-driven analysis and computer vision capabilities is expected to significantly improve Hudl’s existing services, enabling more than 400,000 volleyball users to access key performance data more quickly.

Computer Vision refers to the field of artificial intelligence that enables computers to interpret and process visual information from the world, such as analyzing video footage to extract meaningful data and improving the assessment of performance in various applications.

Similar Israeli companies include: Visionary.ai, SpotReality.

5. Quicklizard Intelligent Pricing Solutions

On February 5, 2025, Riverwood Capital, a U.S. private equity firm, announced its acquisition of the Israeli AI pricing company Quicklizard for approximately $48 million, reflecting a 30% premium on the company's market value. Upon completion of the deal, subject to regulatory approval, Quicklizard will operate as a wholly-owned subsidiary and will be delisted from the Tel Aviv Stock Exchange.

Quicklizard specializes in AI-driven pricing software that synthesizes data from multiple sources to automate and enhance pricing strategies for retailers and manufacturers.

Riverwood Capital focuses on investing in technology companies and aims to leverage Quicklizard's innovative dynamic pricing capabilities to strengthen its portfolio. This acquisition will enable Riverwood to capitalize on the growing demand for automated pricing solutions in the retail sector, enhancing its market position. The integration of Quicklizard's AI Pricing Software is expected to facilitate improved pricing strategies, benefiting both companies as they navigate a competitive landscape.

AI Pricing Software is a technology that uses artificial intelligence algorithms to analyze data from various sources to optimize pricing strategies for businesses. This technology enables manufacturers and retailers to automate their pricing systems across diverse marketing and distribution channels, allowing for dynamic adjustments based on current market conditions.

Dynamic Pricing is a pricing strategy where prices are adjusted in real-time based on market demand, competition, and other external factors. This approach allows businesses to maximize revenue and maintain competitiveness by swiftly responding to fluctuations in the market.

Similar Israeli companies include: Dynamic Yield.

6. LipoVation Lipid-based nano/micro particle therapies

On February 4, 2025, Revium Rx (OTC: RVRC) announced the completion of its acquisition of LipoVation Ltd., an Israeli company focused on lipid-based nano/micro particle medicines. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

LipoVation specializes in developing advanced drug delivery systems utilizing lipid technology, particularly nanoparticle-based therapeutics for complex diseases.

Revium Rx is shifting its strategic focus toward nanoparticle-based therapeutics to enhance drug delivery systems for various medical applications. This acquisition enables Revium Rx to integrate LipoVation’s innovative approaches into its product line, including advanced formulations targeting antibiotic resistance and cancer immunotherapy. The merger allows Revium Rx to leverage LipoVation’s proprietary expertise in nanoparticle therapeutics, potentially expanding its market reach and improving treatment efficacy across multiple therapeutic areas, including oncology and infectious diseases.

Nanoparticle-based therapeutics refer to drug delivery systems that utilize nanoparticles to improve the efficacy and targeted delivery of therapeutic agents. This delivery method leverages the unique properties of nanoparticles to enhance bioavailability, stability, and controlled release, thereby improving treatment outcomes for various diseases, including cancer and antibiotic-resistant infections.

Lipid-based drug delivery refers to the use of lipid formulations to encapsulate and deliver therapeutic agents. This delivery method utilizes the biocompatibility and versatility of lipids to enhance drug solubility, stability, and absorption, particularly for poorly soluble compounds, thereby improving the efficacy of various treatments.

Similar Israeli companies include: NanoGhosts, Nanocarry Therapeutics, PharMedica.
