Israeli merger and acquisition deals

1. Konnecto Partnership intelligence marketing platform

On 29th June 2025, Partnerize, a global partnership automation platform, announced the acquisition of Konnecto, an AI-powered affiliate marketing technology company from Israel. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Konnecto specializes in AI-driven affiliate marketing technology, providing sophisticated solutions for partner discovery, performance optimization, and competitive intelligence. The company’s technology enables brands to leverage artificial intelligence for enhanced data insights and strategic decision-making in affiliate marketing programs.

Partnerize, backed by Accel-KKR, is a leading platform in partnership automation, focusing on modernizing affiliate marketing through technological innovation.

The acquisition introduces AI-powered capabilities that will reshape partnership marketing dynamics. The integration brings advanced partner discovery, predictive fraud and risk scoring, and robust clickstream analytics to Partnerize’s existing platform. This strategic move aims to amplify partnership management through sophisticated AI solutions, enabling clients to identify high-potential partners and optimize performance through data-driven insights. The combined entity is positioning itself to drive significant category growth by moving beyond conventional affiliate models toward an AI-native future.

AI-Powered Insights refer to the use of artificial intelligence technologies to analyze data and derive actionable insights that can inform business strategies and decisions. This includes identifying patterns, trends, and anomalies in data that are challenging to detect manually.

Predictive Fraud and Risk Scoring refers to the use of advanced analytics and machine learning algorithms to assess the likelihood of fraudulent activity and associated risks in real-time. This technology enables organizations to proactively mitigate potential losses by identifying and addressing high-risk transactions before they occur.

Similar Israeli companies include: Insighting.

2. Otterize Cloud-native identity and data security

On 26th June 2025, Israeli cybersecurity unicorn Cyera acquired fellow Israeli company Otterize for tens of millions of dollars, strengthening its cloud data protection capabilities with intent-based access control technology.

Otterize, founded in 2022, specializes in securing non-human identities in cloud environments through a combination of open-source tools and SaaS solutions. The company’s innovative Intent-Based Access Control technology streamlines the process of determining and configuring minimum access requirements for cloud services. The startup has secured $11.5 million in seed funding from notable investors, including Index Ventures and Dig Ventures.

Cyera, following its recent $540 million Series E round at a $6 billion valuation, continues its strategic expansion through acquisitions in Israel, with total investments reaching approximately $250 million. This acquisition represents its third Israeli purchase, following the successful integrations of Trail Security and Shape AI.

The strategic combination of Otterize’s technology enhances Cyera’s cloud data protection portfolio by addressing the critical challenges of identity management and access control in cloud environments. The synergy enables automated security configurations while reducing potential vulnerabilities, positioning Cyera to deliver more comprehensive cloud security solutions to its expanding global customer base.

Intent-Based Access Control (IBAC) is a security mechanism that allows organizations to manage and enforce access to resources based on the intended purpose of access rather than predefined roles. This approach enhances flexibility and security by dynamically adjusting permissions as user needs evolve.

Non-Human Identities refer to identities that are not associated with human users, such as applications, services, or devices that need to authenticate and interact with cloud resources. Managing these identities is crucial for maintaining security in cloud environments.

Similar Israeli companies include: Infinipoint, Astrix Security.

3. Melio Bill payment and invoicing

On 25th June 2025, New Zealand-based Xero (ASX: XRO) agreed to acquire Melio, an Israeli-American fintech startup, in a deal valued at up to USD 3 billion. The transaction includes a USD 2.5 billion upfront payment and USD 500 million in performance-based earnouts over three years.

Melio has established itself as a significant player in the U.S. digital payments landscape, processing over USD 30 billion in transactions and generating USD 187 million in annualized revenue. The company’s innovative payment solutions serve more than 80,000 small businesses, optimizing their bill payment and vendor management workflows.

Xero, a leading accounting software provider listed on the Australian Securities Exchange, has built a strong global footprint in cloud-based financial services. Its platform enables businesses to streamline accounting operations while ensuring compliance with local regulations.

The acquisition significantly enhances Xero’s strategic position in the U.S. market, integrating its robust accounting expertise with Melio’s advanced payment processing capabilities. The merger will create a comprehensive financial platform addressing both accounting and payment needs for small businesses. Melio’s co-founder, Matan Bar, who brings extensive experience from PayPal and Venmo, will lead the combined U.S. operations. The synergy between these complementary services positions the unified entity to capture a substantial share of the expanding digital financial services market.

Accounts Payable and Receivable Solutions (APRS) are financial software tools that assist businesses in efficiently managing their incoming and outgoing payments. These solutions streamline invoice tracking, payment processing, and cash flow management — all crucial for maintaining healthy financial operations.

Payment Processing Systems refer to technology solutions that facilitate electronic transactions between buyers and sellers. These systems handle the authorization, processing, and settlement of payments, ensuring secure and efficient financial exchanges.

Similar Israeli companies include: Tipalti, DirectPay.

4. Cipia In-cabin sensing solutions for automotive safety

On 17th June 2025, HARMAN International, a wholly owned subsidiary of Samsung Electronics Co., Ltd. (South Korea), announced the asset acquisition of Cipia (Israel). The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Cipia is a pioneer in automotive intelligent sensing solutions, specializing in Driver Monitoring Systems (DMS) and Occupancy Monitoring Systems (OMS). The company’s edge-based computer vision and AI technologies enable real-time monitoring of driver behavior and cabin occupancy, enhancing vehicle safety and user experience.

HARMAN International, under Samsung Electronics’ umbrella, is a global leader in automotive technology solutions. The company has already integrated Cipia’s technology into its Ready Care product — the industry’s first system capable of measuring both visual and cognitive driver load in real time. The acquisition accelerates HARMAN’s in-cabin sensing roadmap and strengthens its position in intelligent automotive solutions.

By incorporating Cipia’s AI and computer vision capabilities, HARMAN will enhance its ability to deliver personalized, safety-focused features to global automakers. The integration particularly bolsters HARMAN’s Ready Care product line, enabling more sophisticated driver monitoring and cabin awareness capabilities. While Cipia’s aftermarket business will remain independent, the core technology integration promises to advance the development of smarter, more responsive vehicle systems.

Driver Monitoring System (DMS) is a technology used to monitor the driver’s state, including attention and fatigue levels, through various sensors and algorithms. DMS enhances vehicle safety by providing real-time feedback or alerts when the driver shows signs of distraction or drowsiness.

Occupancy Monitoring System (OMS) is a technology designed to detect and analyze the presence and condition of passengers in a vehicle. This system enhances safety and comfort by adjusting vehicle settings and providing information about passenger status, such as seatbelt usage or potential emergencies.

Similar Israeli companies include: Jungo Software, Advanced Measurement Systems.

5. Statement AI-driven treasury automation solutions

On 18th June 2025, Israeli fintech unicorn Tipalti acquired Statement, a fellow Israeli startup, in a deal valued between USD 35–45 million. The transaction strengthens Tipalti’s position in comprehensive financial management solutions.

Statement, founded in 2022, has developed an AI-powered platform for global cash flow management that equips businesses with real-time visibility and control over their financial operations. The company’s sophisticated solution seamlessly integrates with various financial platforms, including banking systems, ERPs, and billing tools. Statement demonstrated strong market potential by securing USD 12 million in seed funding in 2023.

Tipalti, an established leader in financial automation solutions, serves over 4,000 global customers. The company’s robust platform streamlines accounts payable workflows, facilitates multi-currency transactions, and delivers comprehensive expense management capabilities.

The strategic acquisition combines Statement’s innovative AI-based cash flow management expertise with Tipalti’s proven financial automation infrastructure. This synergistic integration will enhance real-time financial visibility for businesses, streamline manual processes, and deliver advanced cash flow forecasting capabilities. The merger significantly bolsters Tipalti’s ability to offer sophisticated financial management solutions that integrate seamlessly with existing business ecosystems.

AI-Based Cash Flow Management refers to the use of artificial intelligence technologies to automate and optimize the processes involved in managing a company’s cash flow. This includes providing real-time visibility into cash positions, forecasting future cash flows, and offering insights that help businesses make informed financial decisions.

Financial Automation Platform is a comprehensive software solution designed to streamline financial processes through automation. Such platforms typically provide functionalities like invoice management, payment processing, and reporting, thereby minimizing manual workloads and enhancing operational efficiency.

Similar Israeli companies include: Panax Tech, Obol, Cypago.

6. Base44 AI app-building solutions

On 18th June 2025, Wix (NASDAQ: WIX) acquired Base44, an Israeli startup, for USD 80 million in cash, marking a strategic expansion into vibe coding and conversational AI technology. The six-month-old company, founded by Maor Shlomo, has attracted 250,000 users and achieved profitability with USD 189,000 in earnings for May 2025.

Base44’s innovative AI-powered platform enables users to create sophisticated software solutions through natural language interactions, revolutionizing the traditional application development process. The platform features a streamlined, chat-based interface that manages complex technical components including database architecture, user authentication, and deployment automation. Notable partnerships with industry leaders such as eToro and SimilarWeb underscore the platform’s enterprise-grade capabilities.

Wix, a global leader in website development solutions, continues to enhance its position in the AI-driven creation space through strategic acquisitions and technological innovation. The company’s comprehensive platform serves millions of users worldwide, providing intuitive tools for establishing and scaling their digital presence.

This strategic acquisition aligns with the industry’s evolution toward intent-driven software development, where conversational interfaces are becoming the preferred method for application creation. The integration of Base44’s technology significantly enhances Wix’s ability to serve users seeking streamlined digital solutions through natural language interaction, while expanding its market reach to segments requiring advanced development capabilities without technical expertise.

Vibe Coding is a new approach to software development that allows users to create applications by simply expressing their intent through natural language prompts, rather than traditional coding methods. This paradigm shift is facilitated by intelligent agents that manage the technical aspects of application creation, making it accessible to individuals without coding experience.

Conversational AI refers to technologies that enable machines to engage in human-like dialogue, understanding and responding to natural language inputs. This technology is pivotal in tools like Base44, which utilizes a chat-based interface to allow users to build applications using simple text prompts.

Similar Israeli companies include: Chat Leap, Ai Conversation Systems, One AI.

7. Shape AI AI solutions

On 11th June 2025, Cyera, an Israeli cybersecurity company, acquired Shape AI, an Israeli startup, in a deal estimated to be worth tens of millions of dollars, though the specific transaction amount remains undisclosed. This acquisition marks Cyera’s second purchase of an Israeli startup following its USD 162 million acquisition of Trail Security last year.

Shape AI, founded by Daniel Eisenberg, Eden Gaon, and Ariel Schon, had secured USD 5.5 million in funding from IBI’s high-tech fund and Mensch Capital prior to the acquisition. The company initially developed a smart inventory management system for retail chains but later strategically pivoted to focus on cloud infrastructure solutions.

Cyera, having raised USD 60 million in its seed round three years ago, is currently in the process of raising USD 500 million at a USD 6 billion valuation. The company has established itself as a significant player in the cybersecurity landscape, particularly focusing on expanding its technological capabilities through strategic acquisitions.

The acquisition is primarily talent-focused, aimed at integrating Shape AI’s team of experienced technology professionals into Cyera’s operations. This strategic move will enhance Cyera’s capabilities in identity management and cloud activity analysis, though Shape AI’s original product will be discontinued. The integration of Shape AI’s expertise positions Cyera to strengthen its technological innovation and market presence in the rapidly evolving cybersecurity sector.

Identity Management is a framework for business processes that facilitates the management of electronic identities. It encompasses policies, technologies, and tools that allow organizations to ensure that the right individuals (or systems) have the appropriate access to technology resources. This includes user authentication, authorization, and the maintenance of user profiles.

Cloud Activity Analysis refers to the process of monitoring and analyzing activities and behaviors within cloud environments. This includes tracking user interactions, application performance, and resource utilization to enhance security, optimize performance, and ensure compliance with regulations.

Similar Israeli companies include: Aerobase Israel.

8. Autotalks Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication solutions

On 5th June 2025, Qualcomm (NASDAQ: QCOM) finalized the acquisition of Autotalks from Israel, following a year-long delay due to regulatory challenges. The transaction amount has not been disclosed, though earlier discussions valued the deal between USD 350–400 million.

Autotalks, founded in 2009, specializes in Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication chipsets that enable vehicles to share critical data such as speed, location, and braking status with surrounding vehicles and infrastructure. The company has secured USD 150 million in funding and has already landed a significant contract with a major automaker for its advanced chipsets.

Qualcomm, a leading American semiconductor giant, has been expanding its automotive portfolio through its Snapdragon Digital Chassis platform. The company has established itself as a key player in automotive connectivity and infotainment systems, leveraging its expertise in mobile technology and wireless communications.

This acquisition strengthens Qualcomm’s position in the evolving automotive safety landscape, particularly as governments worldwide mandate V2X infrastructure to enhance road safety. The integration of Autotalks’ dual-protocol technology, supporting both Dedicated Short Range Communications (DSRC) and Cellular-V2X, provides Qualcomm with a comprehensive solution for global markets. The timing aligns with the automotive industry’s shift toward advanced safety features, as vehicles equipped with these new chips are expected to hit the roads in 2025.

Vehicle-to-Everything Communication (V2X) is a technology that enables vehicles to communicate with each other and with surrounding infrastructure to enhance safety and efficiency. It allows vehicles to share critical data such as speed, direction, location, and braking status, which can help prevent accidents and improve traffic management.

Dedicated Short Range Communications (DSRC) is a wireless communication protocol specifically designed for automotive applications. It enables low-latency communication between vehicles and infrastructure over short distances, which is critical for real-time safety applications in vehicular networks.

Similar Israeli companies include: Eye-Net Mobile.

9. AudITech IT Audit Automation Solutions

On 4th June 2025, Scytale, based in Israel, acquired AudITech, also from Israel, for USD 15 million to enhance its compliance automation capabilities. The acquisition focuses on integrating AudITech’s SOX-focused platform into Scytale’s AI-driven compliance system.

AudITech specializes in SOX compliance automation, developing solutions that streamline and automate audit processes for organizations. The company has built a reputation for its expertise in simplifying complex compliance requirements through technological innovation.

Scytale, a leading compliance automation provider, has established itself as one of the premier compliance software solutions providers, recently earning recognition among Tekpon’s Top 10 Compliance Software Solutions in 2024. The company combines advanced compliance expertise with cutting-edge automation technology to transform how organizations manage their security compliance requirements.

The integration of AudITech’s specialized SOX compliance platform with Scytale’s existing AI-driven system will create a more comprehensive compliance automation solution. This strategic combination enhances the automated compliance capabilities available to organizations, particularly in managing SOX requirements, while leveraging artificial intelligence to optimize audit processes. The merger strengthens Scytale’s market position in the rapidly expanding compliance automation sector, particularly as it expands its partnerships with Managed Security Service Providers.

Compliance Automation is the use of technology to automate the process of ensuring that an organization adheres to regulatory requirements and internal policies. This involves the integration of tools and platforms that streamline documentation, reporting, and monitoring compliance-related activities, thus reducing manual efforts and increasing accuracy.

SOX Compliance refers to the adherence to the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX), a U.S. law that protects investors by improving the accuracy and reliability of corporate disclosures. The act mandates strict reforms to enhance financial disclosures from corporations and requires all financial reports to include an Internal Controls Report, which is a critical aspect of compliance automation.

Similar Israeli companies include: Regulait.
