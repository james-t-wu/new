Israeli Merger and Acquisition Deals

1. 4M Defense demining and unexploded ordnance clearance technologies

On 27th October 2025, Ondas Holdings Inc. (NASDAQ: ONDS) signed a definitive agreement to acquire a controlling interest in 4M Defense Ltd., an Israeli smart demining company. The transaction value has not been disclosed.

4M Defense specializes in advanced demining technologies, utilizing robotic systems combined with AI-driven ground and subsurface detection capabilities. Its intelligent ground platform integrates satellite imagery, drone data, and historical threat maps.

Ondas Holdings, through its Ondas Autonomous Systems business unit, provides autonomous aerial and ground robotic solutions, and through Ondas Networks, delivers private wireless communication services.

This acquisition expands Ondas' addressable market into the global demining sector. By integrating 4M's subsurface mapping capabilities with Ondas' autonomous drone systems, the combined technology is expected to increase demining speed up to four times compared with traditional methods while significantly improving cost efficiency.

Similar Israeli companies include: Netline Communications Technologies (Electronic warfare and wireless signal countermeasure systems); Metis Augmented Intelligence (Law enforcement and public safety data analytics solutions).

2. Tipulog Cloud-based clinic management solutions

On 24th October 2025, Israeli company Arbox acquired Tipulog, a cloud clinic management platform, for 20 million shekels ($5.4 million). This acquisition follows their recent purchase of the beauty appointment management app "Kavanu" for an undisclosed amount.

Tipulog, founded in 2013, provides comprehensive cloud-based clinic management solutions for over 12,000 healthcare practitioners and doctors in Israel. The platform offers essential tools for managing medical and therapeutic clinics.

Arbox, established in 2016 by childhood friends Allen Davenashon and Ran Lev, has evolved from a simple class registration app into a comprehensive business management platform serving over 6,000 organizations in Israel. The bootstrapped company now employs 50 people at its Ramat Gan headquarters.

The acquisition marks Arbox's strategic expansion into the healthcare sector, complementing its earlier move into the beauty industry. By integrating Tipulog's specialized clinic management capabilities with its existing business management platform, Arbox strengthens its market position across multiple sectors. The company plans three additional acquisitions by the end of next year to support further growth into new business segments.

Cloud Clinic Management is a technology that provides tools and solutions for managing medical clinics and treatment centers through cloud-based systems. It allows healthcare professionals to efficiently manage appointments, patient records, billing, and other administrative tasks in a centralized and accessible manner.

Similar Israeli companies include: DOCTOR Clinix (Clinic and Medical Center Management Solutions).

3. CloudWize Cloud Security and Compliance Solutions

On 23rd October 2025, DoiT (USA) acquired CloudWize (Israel) as part of its USD 250 million investment initiative in AI-driven Cloud Operations (CloudOps) and Financial Operations (FinOps) solutions. The transaction amount was not disclosed.

CloudWize is a multi-cloud security posture and compliance management platform that provides continuous protection across AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure. The platform detects misconfigurations, compliance drifts, and attack paths, while enabling automated remediation through policy-as-code guardrails.

DoiT specializes in enterprise-grade FinOps and CloudOps solutions, offering intelligent cloud financial optimization and management services. The company has expanded its capabilities through recent acquisitions, including PerfectScale and LiveDiagrams, further strengthening its position in the intelligent automation and cloud optimization ecosystem.

This acquisition expands DoiT's Cloud Intelligence capabilities beyond financial management to include security posture, compliance, and automated policy enforcement. By linking security posture insights with cost impact, the combined platform addresses the root causes of cloud operational inefficiencies stemming from misconfigurations and security drift. This integration bridges the gap between FinOps, SecOps, and DevOps teams, enabling organizations to prioritize risks based on business impact and automate remediation workflows. Cloud security monitoring helps prevent issues before they escalate into costly incidents, while automated policy enforcement ensures consistent security implementation across multi-cloud environments.

Cloud Security Posture Management (CSPM) is a type of security solution that helps organizations identify and remediate risks in their cloud environments. CSPM tools continuously monitor cloud resources to ensure compliance with security policies and standards, detect misconfigurations, and provide actionable insights to improve the overall security posture of cloud infrastructures.

Policy-as-Code is the practice of defining and managing security and compliance policies through code, enabling automated enforcement and validation across cloud environments. This approach enhances consistency, scalability, and agility in policy management, allowing organizations to quickly adapt to evolving security requirements and regulatory changes.

Similar Israeli companies include: Algosec (Network security policy management); Slauth.io (Automated Identity and Access Management Solutions).

4. 8fig eCommerce funding solutions

On 21st October 2025, Bizcap, an Australian company, together with its U.S. affiliate NewCo Capital Group, acquired Israeli fintech firm 8fig, which had previously raised USD 75 million in equity funding. The transaction amount was not disclosed.

8fig, founded in 2020, has developed a funding and management platform for e-commerce businesses, providing more than USD 500 million in capital to online sellers through its "AI CFO" platform. The system integrates cash flow, inventory, and supply chain planning tools to help merchants optimize operations and manage growth effectively.

Bizcap is a global non-bank lender specializing in technology-driven business financing, with operations in Australia and the United States through NewCo Capital Group. Leveraging its automated underwriting systems, Bizcap has become a leading provider of capital solutions for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

The acquisition enables 8fig to retain its brand identity and leadership team while gaining access to Bizcap's capital resources, operational expertise, and global network. This strategic move strengthens Bizcap's capabilities in AI-driven financing and intelligent business tools, allowing the combined organization to deliver comprehensive solutions for cash flow management, inventory planning, and growth financing for SMEs. The integration of 8fig's AI technology with Bizcap's lending infrastructure creates a stronger platform for e-commerce businesses seeking to scale globally.

AI CFO is an AI-powered financial management platform designed to assist small and medium-sized enterprises in financial planning, forecasting, and operational scaling. It consolidates key financial tools and delivers insights on cash flow, inventory, and supply chain performance, empowering businesses to make data-driven financial decisions.

Automated Underwriting refers to the use of technology and algorithms to evaluate the risk of lending to a business or insuring a customer without human intervention. This process leverages data analytics and machine learning to assess applications rapidly and accurately, improving efficiency and accuracy in the lending process.

Similar Israeli companies include: Firmbase (Financial planning and analysis solutions); GINIX (Financial and accounting services for SMBs); Pagaya (AI-driven lending and asset management network); A.Z. Smartlend (Big Data-based loan automation decision solutions).

5. NeuroBlade Data analytics acceleration solutions

On 17th October 2025, Amazon's (NASDAQ: AMZN) AWS Annapurna Labs acquired the core engineering team from Israeli data analytics startup NeuroBlade, which had previously raised USD 110 million in funding.

NeuroBlade developed a novel data analytics architecture that integrates computation directly into memory, effectively eliminating traditional data processing bottlenecks.

AWS Annapurna Labs, acquired by Amazon in 2015 for approximately USD 370 million, specializes in advanced chip design and artificial intelligence hardware, including network training processors built for large-scale AI systems.

This strategic acquisition strengthens Amazon's position in the custom silicon and data acceleration market by incorporating NeuroBlade's memory accelerator technology, which integrates proprietary hardware, software, and algorithms to significantly boost computational efficiency. The move aligns with the broader industry trend of major cloud providers developing custom chips to enhance AI and data processing performance. For NeuroBlade, the acquisition represents both validation of its innovative approach and the conclusion of its independent operations, as dozens of its core developers transition to AWS.

Data Analytics Architecture refers to a framework designed to accelerate the processing and analysis of large datasets by embedding computation within memory, thereby removing data transfer bottlenecks. This approach typically combines hardware, software, and algorithmic optimization to create a high-efficiency system for executing complex, large-scale computations.

Memory Accelerator Technology is a system that enhances data processing performance by integrating computation directly into memory, rather than relying solely on conventional CPU- or GPU-based architectures. This technology enables faster data access, lower latency, and higher throughput, making it essential for applications involving high-performance computing (HPC) and large-scale AI workloads.

6. Genda Digital Management Solutions for Building Construction

On 16th October 2025, Buildots (Israel) acquired Genda (USA), marking a significant step toward integrating construction progress tracking with workforce intelligence. The transaction amount was not disclosed.

Genda specializes in job site automation, capturing critical data insights with a focus on workforce monitoring, digital safety management, and resource scheduling. The platform streamlines manual processes and delivers real-time data to support more informed decision-making across construction projects.

Buildots is an AI-powered construction management company that uses helmet-mounted cameras to capture and analyze on-site construction progress. The company has raised a total of USD 166 million in funding, including a recent USD 45 million Series D round, solidifying its position as a market leader in providing real-time insights into construction progress, pace, and delay risk management.

This acquisition addresses a key industry challenge—the fragmentation of progress and workforce data across construction projects. By integrating Genda's workforce management capabilities with Buildots' progress tracking technology, contractors will gain unprecedented visibility into the connection between labor, progress, and productivity. This unified approach to Productivity Intelligence enables construction teams to evaluate delay mitigation effectiveness and leverage comprehensive insights for future project planning.

Genda will continue operations from its Austin, Texas headquarters under the leadership of founder Erez Dror, serving major clients including DPR, Hensel Phelps, and Clark Construction.

Productivity Intelligence refers to the integration and analysis of workforce productivity and construction progress data, enabling organizations to make data-driven decisions that improve project management efficiency and operational performance.

Similar Israeli companies include: Ubeya (Flexible workforce management solutions); SAS (Data Analytics and AI Solutions); EZShift (Employee shift and work scheduling management solutions).

7. Exponet Construction management solutions

On 15th October 2025, Priority Software Ltd. (Israel) has acquired Expo-Net, a developer of advanced cloud platforms for construction management. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Expo-Net specializes in developing cloud-based platforms that enable comprehensive project management for construction and architecture industries. Their system features unique components designed for central control, coordination between stakeholders, and process optimization that drives significant cost savings.

Priority Software is a leading global provider of ERP and business management solutions with a history of strategic acquisitions across various sectors including retail, hospitality, education, and logistics warehouse management. The company offers a flexible and open platform with integrated financial, logistic, and operational modules.

This acquisition strengthens Priority's position in the construction sector, which is experiencing accelerated digitalization worldwide. By integrating Expo-Net's specialized project management capabilities with Priority's comprehensive business systems, construction companies will gain real-time insights connecting on-site operations with core business functions. The combined solution will enable managers to oversee everything from initial planning to execution while maintaining control over supply chains, contractor relationships, budgets, and resources.

A cloud-based management platform is a software solution that leverages cloud computing to provide management services and tools for various applications, enabling users to access, monitor, and control their resources from anywhere via the internet.

Construction Project Management involves the planning, coordination, and control of a construction project from inception to completion. It encompasses various processes such as budgeting, scheduling, resource allocation, and contractor management to ensure that a project is completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

Similar Israeli companies include: GeoCloud (Cloud-based desktop software solutions); HaKaveret (Cloud-based customer relationship management solutions); Beshan Project Initiation and Management (Project Management and Construction Supervision).

8. Cybereason AI-driven cybersecurity solutions

On 14th October 2025, LevelBlue, a provider of managed security services from the United States, has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire Cybereason, an Israeli cybersecurity company once valued at over $3 billion. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Cybereason, founded in 2012 by Lior Div, Yonatan Striem-Amit, and Yossi Naar, is known for its Extended Detection and Response platform and digital forensics capabilities. The company had previously raised hundreds of millions in funding before facing significant challenges, including multiple rounds of layoffs between 2022 and 2024.

LevelBlue, an AT&T spinoff, positions itself as the world's largest pure-play provider of managed security services. This acquisition marks its third deal of 2025, following its earlier acquisition of Trustwave in July.

The strategic combination unites LevelBlue's AI-powered managed detection and response capabilities with Cybereason's XDR platform and incident response services, creating a more comprehensive cybersecurity offering.

Extended Detection and Response (XDR) is a security solution that integrates multiple security products into a cohesive system, providing advanced detection, investigation, and response capabilities across various security layers such as network, endpoint, and server security. XDR enhances threat detection and response times by correlating data from different sources, enabling organizations to respond to threats more effectively.

Cyber Crisis Response is a strategic approach to prepare for, manage, and mitigate the impacts of cyber incidents that can threaten critical systems and data within an organization.

Similar Israeli companies include: Hunters (Cybersecurity and software services); Mitiga (Cloud security threat detection and response); Code Blue (Cyber crisis management and preparedness); Cytactic (Cyber crisis management solutions).

9. Wisdo Health Peer support mental health solutions

On 6th October 2025, Talkspace (NASDAQ: TALK) has acquired Wisdo Health, an AI-powered peer support platform based in the United States. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Wisdo Health is an artificial intelligence-driven platform that provides 24/7 access to trained peers, moderated communities, and group coaching. The company focuses on combating social isolation and loneliness, having helped over one million individuals improve their emotional well-being.

Talkspace is an established online therapy provider offering virtual mental health services. The company previously partnered with Wisdo Health a year ago to improve mental health resource access for the 65-plus community.

This acquisition enables Talkspace to create a comprehensive mental health ecosystem spanning from peer support to clinical therapy and psychiatry. The integration adds lower-acuity options to Talkspace's service line, allowing users to access appropriate support whether they're beginning their mental health journey, supplementing ongoing therapy, or maintaining wellness after treatment. By incorporating Wisdo's peer support technology, Talkspace strengthens its ability to address growing concerns around social connection and emotional resilience in a clinically validated framework.

Peer Support is a system of providing assistance and encouragement to individuals by their peers, often utilized in mental health services to enhance emotional resilience and foster social connections.
