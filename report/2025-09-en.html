Executive Summary

In September 2025, the Israeli technology sector raised approximately USD 3.62 billion in total funding. Investment activity this month ranged from seed rounds to post-IPO financing. Artificial intelligence (AI) and software-as-a-service (SaaS) remained the dominant investment themes, together accounting for more than one-third of all deals. Key growth areas included cybersecurity, industry-specific SaaS, generative AI, and data infrastructure.

The healthcare sector continued to diversify, spanning enzymatic therapeutics, minimally invasive robotics, wearable technologies, and intelligent operating room solutions. Meanwhile, sustainable energy and alternative food sectors advanced in parallel, featuring developments in solar power, renewable energy, and plant-based cheese innovation. Additional activity was recorded in semiconductor metrology and inspection, as well as satellite communications technologies.

In addition, the total value of publicly disclosed acquisitions reached approximately USD 741 million. The acquired Israeli companies include: Fine (AI-powered rapid app development platform), Pelanor (AI-driven financial operations solutions), Intelligo Group (AI-based due diligence and background checks), DigitalOwl (AI-powered structured medical record analysis), Check-Cap (colorectal cancer screening solutions), myInterview (intelligent candidate video screening solutions), Vectorious Medical Technologies (heart failure monitoring and management technology), Firmus (AI-powered construction design risk analysis), Aim Security (enterprise-grade AI security solutions), Sayata (commercial risk automation solutions), Tiltan Software Engineering (3D visual simulation and training solutions), and Acclym / Agritask (agricultural supply chain intelligence solutions).

Israeli Merger and Acquisition Deals in September 2025

1. Fine AI-powered rapid app development platform

On 30th September 2025, HoneyBook, an AI-powered customer relationship management platform based in the United States, announced the acquisition of Fine.dev, a startup specializing in AI-assisted coding and rapid application development. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Fine.dev is an AI application development platform for developers and non-developers, helping users build and launch various web applications within minutes. The platform supports creating AI agents, SaaS products, internal tools, real-time chat applications, and personalized efficiency tools. Fine.dev provides complete backend solutions and has pioneered the innovative "Vibe Coding" approach that can transform business ideas into working software prototypes within minutes, significantly accelerating the software product development process.

As a leading AI-powered business management platform for small business owners, HoneyBook launched AI features in March 2025. The platform has performed excellently, with user project bookings increasing 2.5 times and saving over 20 hours of administrative work per week.

This acquisition brings professional AI technical support to HoneyBook's product development, enabling faster deployment of personalized tools and workflows. By integrating Fine.dev's Vibe Coding technology, entrepreneurs can transform ideas into practical software tools without traditional programming. This strategic move strengthens HoneyBook's transformation into a fully agentic system where AI can independently coordinate various tools to respond to member needs, ultimately allowing small businesses to customize applications and workflows based on their specific requirements.

Vibe Coding is an emerging method in software development where AI can understand business concepts and transform them into fully functional software prototypes within minutes, enabling entrepreneurs to rapidly develop applications based on concepts.

AI-Assisted Development refers to the use of AI technology in the software development process to help developers create applications more efficiently by automating repetitive tasks and providing intelligent insights throughout the development lifecycle.

Similar Israeli companies include: SpiralSoft Technologies (Software Development and Business Incubation).

2. Pelanor AI-driven Financial Operations solutions

On 28th September 2025, Israeli technology startup Kela announced the acquisition of Israeli AI company Pelanor, with the acquisition amount expected to be tens of millions of dollars. Market reports also suggested the transaction amount could be between USD 20 million and USD 30 million.

Pelanor, founded in late 2022, has developed an AI-based platform that helps organizations analyze and optimize cloud computing spending through real-time monitoring, anomaly detection, and automated resource allocation.

Kela, founded in July 2024, has raised USD 100 million from well-known investment institutions including Sequoia, Lux Capital, and In-Q-Tel. The company is developing an advanced technology integration platform that enables seamless integration of commercial technologies with existing infrastructure systems.

By integrating Pelanor's AI expertise and talent pool, Kela has enhanced its ability to process real-time data from multiple sources including sensor networks and surveillance systems. This integration leverages advanced data analytics and automation capabilities to further strengthen Kela platform's ability to provide unified situational awareness.

AI-Based Platform refers to a technology platform that utilizes artificial intelligence to analyze data and provide insights or automated solutions. For Pelanor, its platform focuses on helping organizations analyze cloud computing spending in real-time and reduce costs through intelligent recommendations and automation.

3. Intelligo Group AI-driven due diligence and background checks

On 22nd September 2025, Carrick Capital Partners, a US-based growth investment firm, announced a majority investment in Intelligo, an Israeli background check solutions provider. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

As a global leading provider of high-precision background check and continuous monitoring solutions, Intelligo primarily serves top global financial institutions. The company's competitive advantage lies in combining human expertise with advanced technology, especially possessing patented deterministic entity resolution technology and over 100 proprietary AI models.

Carrick Capital Partners specializes in investing in software and software-enabled businesses, with an excellent investment record in scaling technology service companies. The firm has extensive experience in the risk and compliance sector, with this investment jointly participated by AB Private Credit Investors and Trinity Capital.

This investment will help Intelligo advance product innovation and market expansion. The combination of Carrick's operational expertise and Intelligo's technological strengths will enhance the company's continuous subject monitoring capabilities and consolidate its market position in the compliance sector. Both parties will focus on developing audit-ready transparency features and enhancing AI-driven verification systems to meet the growing demand for reliable compliance solutions in high-risk investment decisions.

Continuous Subject Monitoring is an ongoing process of evaluating and monitoring individuals or entities to ensure compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) regulatory requirements. Such monitoring helps organizations identify potential risks and maintain real-time updated customer information, thereby improving decision-making process quality.

Deterministic Entity Resolution refers to the process of accurately identifying and matching entities based on predefined rules and attributes, ensuring that information pointing to the same entity in different data records can be precisely associated. This technology is crucial in data management and compliance, significantly improving the reliability and accuracy of background check and monitoring solutions.

Similar Israeli companies include: Cyber Observer (Continuous Control Monitoring solutions); AdvanScreen (KYC and due diligence solutions).

4. DigitalOwl AI-based structured medical record analysis

On 25th September 2025, Datavant announced the acquisition of DigitalOwl, an Israeli pioneer in AI-powered medical data analysis. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

DigitalOwl specializes in AI-powered medical data extraction and analysis, providing efficient medical record review services for the legal and insurance industries. The company's technology can automatically classify and annotate unstructured medical data and won the 2025 "Natural Language Generation Platform of the Year for Medical Record Reviews" award.

As a trusted provider of medical data collaboration platforms, Datavant is committed to ensuring secure and compliant exchange of medical information. Its platform provides services including managed retrieval, automated summarization, and AI-powered predictive analytics for healthcare industry stakeholders.

This acquisition aims to combine DigitalOwl's proprietary technology with Datavant's extensive healthcare provider network to optimize medical record review processes. This strategic integration will address efficiency issues in current medical record reviews that affect case resolution and claims approval. The enhanced platform will optimize data extraction while maintaining strict compliant data exchange protocols, providing automated summarization and predictive analytics services.

AI-Driven Medical Data Analysis refers to the process of using AI technology to analyze medical data (especially unstructured data) to streamline processes such as medical record reviews. This technology helps improve efficiency, reduce errors, and provide actionable insights for the legal and insurance industries.

Natural Language Generation (NLG) is a branch of AI focused on converting structured data into human-readable text. In medical data, NLG can be used to automatically generate summaries and reports from complex medical records, improving the speed and accuracy of data interpretation.

Similar Israeli companies include: Imagestore (Data Processing and Management Solutions); ODMachine (Healthcare Data Analytics and Solutions).

5. Check-Cap Colorectal cancer screening solutions

On 15th September 2025, Israeli medical technology company Check-Cap (NASDAQ: CHEK) announced a definitive merger agreement with MBody AI. According to the agreement terms, MBody AI shareholders will hold 90% of the combined entity, while Check-Cap shareholders will retain 10%. The transaction amount has not been disclosed.

Check-Cap is a medical technology company focusing on non-invasive imaging solutions, primarily developing innovative bowel screening technology. Since 2023, the company has been evaluating strategic transformation options while working to meet Nasdaq's minimum shareholder equity compliance requirements.

MBody AI develops proprietary AI technology, described as the "brain of autonomy," enabling intelligent systems to learn and adapt in real-time. The company initially focused on the hospitality services sector, planning to expand its solutions to warehousing, office management, and healthcare fields.

This merger signifies the company's strategic transformation into the rapidly developing field of embodied AI while preserving Check-Cap's medical technology expertise. The combined entity will be renamed MBody AI Ltd. and will retain Check-Cap's important assets, including patents and proprietary medical equipment. This integration aims to achieve synergies between autonomous workforce solutions and medical technology, particularly leveraging Check-Cap's smart kitchen franchise rights in New Jersey. The transaction is subject to approval at the October 2025 annual general meeting, with the company planning additional private placement financing simultaneously.

Embodied AI refers to AI systems that can interact with the physical world and exhibit intelligent behavior through physical existence. These AI systems aim to learn and adapt in real-time, capable of performing tasks autonomously in multiple fields including hospitality services, warehousing, and healthcare.

Non-invasive Imaging Solutions refers to medical imaging technologies that can visualize internal body structures without surgery. These technologies are crucial for diagnosis and treatment planning, providing key health information without harming patients.

Similar Israeli companies include: Intuition Robotics (Elderly companion technology); Resonai (Smart digital twin solutions); IR-MED (Non-invasive biomarker analysis technology).

7. Vectorious Medical Technologies Heart Failure Monitoring and Management Technology

On 4th September 2025, Edwards Lifesciences (NYSE: EW) completed the acquisition of the remaining 48% equity in Israeli Vectorious Medical Technologies for USD 497 million. Previously in 2023, Edwards had acquired 52% of the company.

Founded in 2011 and emerging from the RAD Biomed incubator, Vectorious Medical Technologies has developed an innovative heart failure monitoring solution. The company's breakthrough V-LAP system is a battery-free sensor implanted in the heart's left atrium, with clinical trials showing the system can reduce patient readmission rates by 85%.

Edwards Lifesciences, a global leader in structural heart disease treatment with approximately 14,000 employees, is strategically expanding its presence in Israel's medical technology field, following last year's USD 300 million acquisition of Involve Biomedical.

This integration of Vectorious's V-LAP technology will further solidify Edwards' position in the heart failure management market. The system can wirelessly transmit left atrial pressure data, enabling real-time treatment adjustments and patient self-management through mobile applications, representing a significant advancement in remote cardiac monitoring. This acquisition also aligns with trends in minimally invasive cardiac treatments and remote patient monitoring solutions.

V-LAP System is a miniature cardiac pressure sensor implant device specifically for remote monitoring of cardiac function and congestion status in chronic heart failure patients. The device measures left atrial pressure and transmits data wirelessly, helping real-time adjustment of treatment plans and improving patients' self-management capabilities of the disease.

Similar Israeli companies include: CardiacSense (Continuous cardiac monitoring solutions).

8. FIRMUS Construction design risk analysis

On 4th September 2025, German software company Nemetschek Group (ETR: NEM) announced through its US subsidiary Bluebeam the acquisition of Israeli-American startup Firmus AI. While specific transaction amount was not disclosed, industry estimates put the scale at tens of millions of dollars.

Firmus AI has a 35-person team in Israel and the United States, developing an AI-driven preconstruction design review and financial risk analysis platform. Its flagship products AI-REVIEW and AI-MATCH analyze 2D PDF drawings to identify design defects, missing information, and cross-disciplinary inconsistencies. Prior to the acquisition, the company had raised USD 11.5 million in funding.

Nemetschek Group is a leading software solution provider for the architecture, engineering, and construction (AEC) industry, with revenue approaching EUR 1 billion in 2024. The company is also listed on Germany's MDAX and TecDAX indices.

Integrating Firmus's AI technology into Bluebeam's widely used PDF workflows marks an important step toward automation in construction risk detection. This strategic acquisition enables Nemetschek to enhance AI capabilities across the global construction field. Firmus is expected to achieve mid-single-digit million euro annual recurring revenue by 2026. Firmus's advanced drawing analysis capabilities combined with Bluebeam's market position are poised to revolutionize how construction teams identify and mitigate potential errors in the preconstruction phase.

AI-Review is a technology that utilizes artificial intelligence to analyze 2D PDF drawings in construction projects. It can identify missing information, scope gaps, and cross-disciplinary inconsistencies, helping construction teams identify costly errors early.

AI-Match is an AI-based tool for matching design elements in construction documents. It ensures design consistency and coordination across various project stages, reducing the risk of discrepancies.

Similar Israeli companies include: Locusview (Utility Construction Digital Management Solutions); Bimmatch (Intelligent optimization solutions for construction projects).

9. Aim Security Enterprise-grade AI security solutions

On 3rd September 2025, Israeli SASE leader Cato Networks announced the acquisition of Israeli AI security company Aim Security, with transaction amount not disclosed.

Founded in 2022 with investment support from YL Ventures and Canaan Partners, Aim Security is a pioneer in enterprise AI security. The company's platform provides comprehensive protection for Fortune 500 and Forbes Global 2000 enterprises across public AI applications, private AI deployments, and AI development lifecycles.

Cato Networks is a cloud-native SASE platform provider with annual recurring revenue exceeding USD 300 million. The company recently received an additional USD 50 million investment from Acrew Capital, bringing its Series G funding total to USD 409 million. Cato has built the first cloud-native SASE platform specifically designed to protect enterprise network traffic.

This acquisition represents Cato Networks' strategic expansion in AI security, combining Aim's advanced AI security solutions with Cato's comprehensive network visualization and policy enforcement infrastructure. The integrated technology stack will address emerging security challenges in AI transformation, especially in protecting enterprise data and AI agent/model interactions. Related enhanced features are expected to be fully integrated into the Cato SASE cloud platform in early 2026, providing advanced AI security posture management and AI firewall protection.

AI Security Posture Management (AI-SPM) is a comprehensive method for managing AI system security throughout the lifecycle, covering AI model and application development, deployment, and operation phases. AI-SPM ensures AI systems maintain compliance while resisting various vulnerabilities and threats.

AI Firewall is a security solution specifically designed to protect AI applications and agents from runtime attacks and vulnerabilities. It monitors AI interactions and detects malicious activities, ensuring AI systems operate safely within their intended environments.

Similar Israeli companies include: APEX (Generative AI Security Solutions); Salt Security (Machine learning-based API security solutions).

10. Sayata Commercial risk automation solutions

On 3rd September 2025, Penn-America Underwriters, a subsidiary of US-based Global Indemnity Group, LLC (NYSE: GBLI), announced the acquisition of US-Israeli AI insurance agency Sayata, with transaction amount not disclosed.

Founded in 2017, Sayata evolved from a cybersecurity data analytics provider to an independent insurance agency operating an AI-driven digital distribution platform. The company's current annual recurring revenue from agency fees is approximately USD 10 million, with expected total annual revenue between USD 60 million and USD 70 million. Its platform provides distribution services for various insurance products including cybersecurity insurance, professional liability insurance, and third-party liability insurance for small and medium enterprises.

Penn-America Underwriters belongs to Global Indemnity Group with a market capitalization of USD 435.3 million, conducting business through multiple specialized agencies and insurance service providers. This acquisition marks the company's third strategic investment in the insurance technology field this year.

This acquisition will enhance Penn-America's digital capabilities while expanding Sayata's market influence through its extensive distribution network. Integration of Sayata's advanced technology platform is expected to optimize commercial property/casualty insurance distribution processes, improving operational efficiency for insurance practitioners. This technology upgrade aligns with Global Indemnity's "Project Manifest" strategy, further enhancing digital capabilities and customer relationship management levels while maintaining Sayata's existing economic model.

AI-Enabled Digital Distribution Marketplace is a platform that utilizes AI technology to facilitate insurance product transactions, optimizing insurance agencies' distribution processes through data analysis, improving operational efficiency and strengthening customer relationship management.

Insurance Agency Operations Platform is a comprehensive software solution designed for insurance agencies to manage core business operations such as policies, claims, and customer relationships. The platform provides necessary tools for insurance agents to simplify workflows and improve service quality.

Similar Israeli companies include: Novidea (Digital insurance distribution solutions); MediMe (AI-driven health insurance comparison solutions).

11. Tiltan Software Engineering 3D visual simulation and training solutions

On 2nd September 2025, Nukkleus Inc. (Nasdaq: NUKK) announced the acquisition of Israeli company Tiltan Software Engineering Ltd. for USD 14 million through cash and stock payments, entering the AI market.

Tiltan Software Engineering has over 30 years of professional experience in the aerospace and defense fields, specializing in developing AI-driven simulation platforms, navigation systems in GPS signal-restricted environments, and synthetic data generation solutions. The company maintains deep cooperation with major Israeli contractors.

Nukkleus Inc. positions itself as a strategic acquirer and developer of high-growth enterprises in the aerospace and defense industry, dedicated to transforming potential companies into market leaders through operational expertise and strategic capital investment.

By integrating Tiltan's advanced simulation platforms and navigation systems, Nukkleus's position in autonomous system development and immersive training environments will be further strengthened. This transaction will enhance both parties' capabilities in developing next-generation solutions, especially in complex environments requiring secure navigation.

GPS-Denied Navigation Systems are technologies that provide navigation support in environments where global positioning system signals are unavailable or unreliable. AI-Driven Simulation Platforms are advanced technology systems that utilize AI to create realistic simulation scenarios.
