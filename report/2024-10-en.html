Israeli merger and acquisition deals

1. Ctrl, Sales workflow automation solutions

On October 30, 2024, Sana announced a $55 million investment round at a $500 million valuation, alongside its acquisition of workflow automation startup CTRL. The funding round was led by NEA, with participation from Menlo Ventures and other investors, bringing Sana's total funding to over $130 million.

Sana is a Swedish AI scaleup focused on enhancing enterprise knowledge accessibility through artificial intelligence. Its platform allows businesses to index, create, and retrieve knowledge efficiently, catering to clients such as Merck and Hinge Health.

CTRL specializes in workflow automation solutions that streamline operational processes for businesses. Backed by investors including LocalGlobe and Earlybird, CTRL has garnered trust from prominent clients like Stripe and Snowflake.

The acquisition of CTRL is strategically significant for Sana as it enhances its capabilities in automating complex workflows, thereby expanding its service offerings. Additionally, the introduction of new Agentic AI features reflects Sana's commitment to providing enterprises with advanced user interfaces for AI, enabling organizations to build custom agents that can tackle multifaceted problems. By integrating CTRL's technology, Sana aims to improve efficiency and productivity across its client base, further solidifying its position as a leader in enterprise AI solutions.

Similar Israeli companies include: Beam (Intelligent Process Automation Solutions); One AI (Conversational AI growth platform); Pragma (Enterprise knowledge management solutions); Ask-AI (Enterprise productivity enhancement solutions).

2. Trail Security, Data protection solutions

On October 17, 2024, Cyera, a cybersecurity firm based in the U.S. and founded in Israel, announced its acquisition of Trail Security for $162 million. This acquisition follows Cyera's recent fundraising efforts, aimed at achieving a pre-money valuation of approximately $3 billion.

Trail Security is a startup currently operating in stealth mode, specializing in data loss prevention solutions that help organizations protect sensitive information from unauthorized access and breaches. Its technology is designed to enhance an organization's security framework and mitigate risks associated with data exposure.

Cyera, recognized for its innovative approach to data security posture management, leverages artificial intelligence to provide organizations with insights into their data security vulnerabilities and compliance requirements. With this acquisition, Cyera aims to bolster its product offerings and address the increasing demands for robust cybersecurity measures in a competitive landscape.

The integration of Trail Security's expertise in data loss prevention will significantly enhance Cyera's capabilities, particularly as organizations face growing threats to their data integrity. This strategic move not only strengthens Cyera's market position but also underscores its commitment to continuous innovation in the cybersecurity space, ensuring it remains competitive against other leading firms.

Similar Israeli companies include: ITsMine (Next-gen data protection solutions); Digital Confidence (Hidden Data Removal and Data Leakage Prevention); Laminar (Data Security Posture Management Services); Polyrize (Automated data security solutions).

3. SipNose, Non-invasive intranasal drug delivery solutions

Aptar Pharma has acquired the Israeli drug delivery company SipNose for approximately $12.5 million, focusing on enhancing its capabilities in the drug delivery market. The acquisition involves the device technology assets of SipNose, which specializes in innovative delivery systems for pharmaceuticals.

Founded in 2011 and based in Yokneam, Israel, the company focuses on developing a nasal drug delivery system that specifically targets areas of the nasal cavity. This technology allows for improved systemic, local, and direct-to-brain delivery of medications, providing a promising alternative to traditional drug delivery methods.

Aptar Pharma, a leading U.S. company in drug delivery solutions, aims to strengthen its portfolio through this acquisition. The strategic significance of this deal lies in SipNose's ability to penetrate the Blood-Brain Barrier, which could open new pathways for non-invasive drug administration. By integrating SipNose’s innovative delivery systems, Aptar Pharma is well-positioned to expand its offerings and enhance patient outcomes in the pharmaceutical industry.

Similar Israeli companies include: SipNose (Non-invasive intranasal drug delivery solutions); Nasus Pharma Ltd. (Powder-based intranasal drug delivery solutions); Nano Carry (Therapeutics development for CNS diseases); Nextar Chempharma Solutions (Drug delivery and formulation development).
